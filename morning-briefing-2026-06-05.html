Section 1 — Intelligence Feed

Policy / Regulatory

FCC pole-attachment rules now in force — new make-ready timelines and self-help contractor approvals live since May 8

20/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: The FCC's revised pole-attachment rules took effect May 8, 2026, imposing firm processing timelines on pole owners and permitting attachers to deploy qualified contractors for surveys, engineering, and make-ready in defined circumstances. NTIA has also extended FCC pole rules to certain co-op and municipal poles for BEAD subgrantees.

Why it matters: Aerial make-ready is the single largest schedule risk on Gigapower greenfield builds; enforceable timelines plus self-help convert a passive bottleneck into a lever.

Action: Direct Engineering and Permitting to re-paper every open pole-attachment application in AZ, NV, and PA against the new federal timelines this week and pre-qualify a self-help make-ready contractor bench before Q3 builds load.

BEAD restructured — "fiber-first" dropped, 54 states/territories approved, 52 funded as construction window opens

18/22
Policy / Regulatory🟡 This Week

What happened: NTIA's restructured BEAD guidance dropped the original fiber-first preference, opening subsidy dollars to fixed wireless and LEO. 54 states/territories have final-proposal approval, 52 have funded award agreements, and major construction runs 2026–2030.

Why it matters: Subsidized FWA/LEO entrants will now overbuild rural-edge passings Gigapower might otherwise have anchored, compressing the addressable wholesale demand in subsidy-eligible census blocks.

Action: Task Wholesale BD to overlay funded BEAD subgrantee maps against AL and FL candidate footprints before site selection locks, and steer greenfield toward unsubsidized competitive blocks.

Nevada locks $417M BEAD allocation; provisional awards to SkyFiber and Stimulus Technologies in Gigapower's home state

19/22
Policy / Regulatory🟡 This Week

What happened: Nevada finalized a $417M BEAD allocation with provisional awards including SkyFiber Internet ($57.5M / 4,684 locations) and Stimulus Technologies ($36.5M / 3,361 locations). Awards target rural and edge locations across the state.

Why it matters: Subsidized builders adjacent to Las Vegas/Clark County change which fringe passings are still competitive for an unsubsidized open-access build and which tenant ISPs have a subsidized alternative.

Action: Have Wholesale BD map SkyFiber and Stimulus award polygons against the Clark County expansion plan this week and flag any overlap to Engineering before the next pass-cost model.

Pennsylvania BEAD skews to wired fiber with Comcast and Verizon leading — competitive pressure on NE PA cluster

19/22
Policy / Regulatory🟡 This Week

What happened: Pennsylvania's broadband office held fiber-to-the-premises as the preferred technology; state awards lean heavily to wired networks with Comcast and Verizon capturing the largest fiber/HFC shares.

Why it matters: Scranton and Wilkes-Barre sit inside a market where incumbents are getting subsidized to harden fiber, raising the bar for tenant-ISP take rates on a Gigapower open-access build in Luzerne/Lackawanna.

Action: Direct the Intelligence effort on the NE PA cluster to pull the PA award shapefiles for Luzerne and Lackawanna and quantify subsidized-incumbent overlap before advancing either market past Intelligence stage.

Supply Chain

"Perfect storm" in fiber supply — G.652.D up 75% since January, seven-year high, as AI data centers and Meta's $6B Corning deal drain capacity

21/22
Supply Chain🔴 Act Today

What happened: Standard single-mode G.652.D fiber rose 75% in January 2026 to a seven-year high as hyperscale/AI loads and Meta's up-to-$6B Corning commitment soak up capacity. Tariffs and Build America, Buy America compliance further tighten available domestic supply and stretch lead times.

Why it matters: Cable is the largest material line in a greenfield pass-cost; a 75% price move plus lead-time slippage directly threatens 2026–2027 build budgets and the timeline to the 1.5M-passing target.

Action: Direct Engineering and Procurement to lock BABA-compliant fiber allocation contracts for the full AZ/NV/PA 2026–2027 build plan now, before further price moves, and stress-test pass-cost models at +75% cable to protect the JV/Parent capital plan.

Section 3 — Technology & Growth Strategy

Nokia ships co-existence line card running 10G, 25G and 50G PON on one fiber — eases the multi-gig upgrade path

16/22
Technology🟢 Monitor

What happened: Nokia launched a high-density 25G PON line card that lets 10G, 25G, and 50G PON co-exist on the same fiber, so an operator on XGS-PON can offer 25G residential and 50G enterprise without a forklift upgrade. ALLO and Brightspeed have tested 50G with Calix; Adtran and Netomnia ran the first UK commercial 50G PON.

Why it matters: Co-existence hardware lets a wholesale operator support tenant ISPs at different speed tiers on one OLT, protecting the "build once" economics central to Gigapower's open-access pitch.

Action: Have Engineering confirm Gigapower's OLT roadmap supports 25G/50G co-existence line cards so tenant ISPs can differentiate by tier without per-tenant fiber, and fold the capability into the Section 4 tenant pitch.
