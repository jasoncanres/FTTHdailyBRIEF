Policy / Regulatory

FCC "Build America" NPRM proposes 120-day ROW shot-clock, fee caps, and a commingling presumption that protects wholesale builds

22/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: At its June 25 open meeting the FCC released a draft NPRM, "Build America: Eliminating Barriers to Wireline Deployments," establishing a rebuttable presumption that a jurisdiction violates Section 253 if it fails to act on a wireline ROW authorization within 120 days, capping permit fees at the government's "actual and direct" cost of managing the ROW, and barring localities from slowing a permit because the provider commingles telecom with non-telecom services over the same fiber.

Why it matters: The commingling presumption is tailor-made for Gigapower's open-access model — it strips municipalities of the argument that a wholesale-plus-tenant network falls outside protected "telecommunications," and the 120-day clock compresses permitting variance across every greenfield market.

Action: Direct Government Affairs to draft Gigapower reply comments before the FCC docket window closes, anchored on the commingling and 120-day provisions; have Permitting map current AZ/NV/PA ROW approval cycle times against the proposed 120-day clock this week to quantify the upside.

NTIA clears 54 states/territories on restructured BEAD; "fiber-first" gone, LEO and fixed wireless now eligible

18/22
Policy / Regulatory🟡 This Week

What happened: The June 8 BEAD Progress Dashboard shows 54 of 56 eligible entities with NTIA-approved Final Proposals, 52 NIST-approved with funds available, and 52 award agreements signed. June guidance dropped the original fiber-first stipulation plus labor, employment, and climate-reporting requirements, opening subsidized dollars to LEO satellite and fixed wireless.

Why it matters: Subsidized FWA and LEO now compete for the same rural and edge passings Gigapower's tenant ISPs would target, but the deregulated build rules also lower Gigapower's own compliance drag in any BEAD-adjacent greenfield.

Action: Have Wholesale BD pull the approved Final Proposals for AZ, NV, PA, AL, and FL by Friday and flag any census blocks where a funded FWA/LEO award now overlaps a planned Gigapower passing — feed conflicts into the market-prioritization model.

Pennsylvania BEAD: $1.16B allocated, $793M provisionally awarded — Comcast and Verizon split nearly half

17/22
Policy / Regulatory🟡 This Week

What happened: Pennsylvania's BEAD allocation totals $1.16B with provisional awards of $793.5M skewed heavily to wired technology; Comcast and Verizon together account for roughly half the state's funding, concentrating subsidized fiber and HFC builds in the incumbents' favor.

Why it matters: Subsidized incumbent builds in Luzerne and Lackawanna counties would harden the overbuild economics for Gigapower's NE PA intelligence cluster (Wilkes-Barre / Scranton) before a single permit is pulled.

Action: Have Wholesale BD cross-reference the PA provisional award map against the Wilkes-Barre/Scranton dossier this week; if Comcast/Verizon hold the funded footprint, pivot the NE PA thesis toward tenant-ISP demand aggregation rather than greenfield overbuild.

FCC's Rapid Broadband Assessment Team issues "first-of-its-kind" pole order in 60 days, siding with attacher on replacement cost

16/22
Policy / Regulatory🟡 This Week

What happened: The FCC fast-tracked a Comcast pole-attachment dispute, issuing an order within 60 days that shifts pole-replacement cost allocation toward the pole owner to protect BEAD timelines — a signal the Commission will move quickly on make-ready and cost disputes that delay deployment.

Why it matters: Where Gigapower contemplates aerial construction, a faster federal escalation path on pole-replacement cost directly de-risks make-ready budgets and schedule in joint-use territory.

Action: Have Engineering identify every planned aerial segment in AZ/NV/PA, confirm the pole owner and whether Section 224 rates apply, and pre-stage the FCC RBAT escalation template as the standard response to any make-ready cost dispute over 60 days old.

Supply Chain

Corning locks multibillion-dollar Amazon data-center fiber deal; AI demand to consume ~30% of global fiber by 2027

17/22
Supply Chain🟡 This Week

What happened: On June 8 Corning and Amazon announced a multiyear, multibillion-dollar agreement for optical fiber, cable, and connectivity across Amazon's U.S. data centers — stacking on a $6B Meta commitment and a May NVIDIA deal to expand Corning's U.S. capacity 50%. Data-center fiber demand grew ~76% YoY in 2025 and is projected to hit ~30% of global demand by 2027, up from under 5% in 2024.

Why it matters: Hyperscalers are pre-buying fiber capacity at a scale that lengthens lead times and hardens pricing for FTTH builders, threatening construction schedules in every Gigapower market simultaneously.

Action: Have Construction and Procurement re-confirm 2026–2027 fiber and conduit lead times with primary suppliers within 10 days and lock allocation under fixed-price terms before hyperscaler demand absorbs the next capacity tranche.

Competitive Intelligence

AT&T signs four open-access wholesale agreements — Boldyn, Digital Infrastructure Group, PRIME FiBER, Ubiquity

18/22
Competitive / Wholesale Peer🟡 This Week

What happened: AT&T struck wholesale access deals with four commercial open-access providers, with initial builds at Texas military bases (Boldyn), Florida (PRIME FiBER), and Minnesota (Ubiquity, which will also build exclusive AT&T greenfield across a multi-state footprint). The agreements let AT&T offer AT&T Fiber and 5G over third-party networks "capex-light."

Why it matters: AT&T — Gigapower's own anchor tenant and JV parent — is diversifying its wholesale supply across competing open-access platforms, including PRIME FiBER in Florida where Gigapower is in early intelligence. That dilutes Gigapower's exclusivity as AT&T's preferred third-party rail.

Action: Escalate to JV/Parent liaison this week: get clarity from AT&T on how Gigapower is prioritized versus PRIME FiBER in overlapping Florida geographies before Gigapower commits build capital to any FL municipality.

Market Trends

Cable broadband ARPU set to decline in 2026 as FWA at $35–$50 undercuts cable's ~$74 weighted average

13/22
Market Trends🟢 Monitor

What happened: Analysts project cable operators will see broadband ARPU decline in 2026, with fixed wireless — priced at $35–$50 versus cable's ~$74 weighted-average ARPU — the primary catalyst for pricing pressure, forcing MSOs to choose between defending ARPU and defending share.

Why it matters: Cable on the defensive is the opening for tenant-ISP fiber take-rate gains on the Gigapower network, but FWA's price floor caps the ARPU ceiling tenant ISPs can underwrite in any market.

Action: Have Wholesale BD model tenant-ISP take-rate scenarios against a $35–$50 FWA price floor in each active market and confirm the wholesale rate card still clears tenant-ISP unit economics at that ceiling.

Section 5 — My Recommendation

File Gigapower reply comments on the FCC's "Build America" wireline NPRM this week and operationalize the 120-day ROW clock now. This is the single highest-leverage action on the board. The draft NPRM's commingling presumption — barring localities from slowing a permit because the network carries non-telecom traffic — is a direct federal shield for the open-access wholesale model, and the 120-day shot-clock plus actual-cost fee cap would compress the permitting variance that currently makes every new municipality a custom negotiation. Government Affairs should put Gigapower on the record championing both provisions while Permitting benchmarks current AZ, NV, and PA approval cycle times against the proposed clock to quantify the schedule and capital upside. Everything else this week — the Corning supply squeeze, AT&T's four new open-access partners diluting Gigapower's exclusivity in Florida, PA's incumbent-heavy BEAD awards — is a reaction to someone else's move. This is the one place Gigapower can shape the rules of the game before they harden, and the comment window will not wait.
