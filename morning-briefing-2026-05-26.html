Utilities formally push back on FCC's 30-day pole contractor approval rule — AEP, Duke, Entergy, Ameren, CenterPoint, Oncor, Alabama Power meet FCC staff

21/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: Seven major investor-owned electric utilities met with FCC staff in late May 2026 to flag contractor backlogs and rising construction costs as obstacles to the FCC's July 2025 pole attachment order. Alabama Power's presence is notable — direct overlap with Gigapower's AL footprint.

Why it matters: The pushback signals utilities will slow-walk the 30-day approval window that took effect May 8. Gigapower's pole-dependent builds in PA (Luzerne/Lackawanna) and any aerial work in AL/FL are the precise use case the new rule was meant to protect.

FCC pole attachment rules take effect May 8 — utilities can no longer bill broadband builders for pre-existing pole defects

20/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: The July 2025 FCC pole order published in the Federal Register May 7 and took effect May 8. It establishes 30-day contractor approval, bans utility cost-shifting of pre-existing safety/structural violations onto attachers, and applies to BEAD builds on co-op and municipal poles per NTIA's April 2026 alignment.

Why it matters: Material make-ready cost reduction on every aerial route Gigapower contemplates. The NTIA cross-reference also pulls co-op/muni poles into FCC jurisdiction — relevant for AL and rural FL where co-op poles dominate.

Action: Engineering reissues outstanding make-ready cost estimates from PPL (PA) and Alabama Power within 14 days under the new rule, demanding utility reabsorption of any line items tied to pre-existing defects. Net savings should be visible by Q3.
