BEAD Program officially operational — $42.45B unlocked, ~65% earmarked for fiber

🔴 Act Today 22/22
Policy/Regulatory
What happened
As of April 30, 2026, NTIA's "Benefit of the Bargain" restructure of BEAD has flipped from planning to deployment phase. State broadband offices are now releasing awards; construction starts H2 2026. The technology mix locks in fiber as the primary delivery vehicle — ~65% of locations to fiber, 20.6% LEO, 11.2% licensed FWA, 2.2% HFC, 0.9% unlicensed FWA.
Why it matters
This is the single biggest funded greenfield opportunity in U.S. broadband history and the LEO carve-out is now the primary competitive vector for unserved territory.
Recommended action
Pull the SC, GA, NC, AL, FL provisional award lists this week. Map every fiber-allocated CAI and unserved cluster within 25 miles of your existing or planned plant. File for any subgrant or partnership window still open in your state office before Q2 close — most state portals lock late-stage applications by mid-summer.

FCC adopts modernized satellite spectrum sharing — opens door for 8x NGSO concurrency

🟡 This Week 18/22
Policy/Regulatory
What happened
The FCC's April 30 open meeting adopted the Modernizing Spectrum Sharing for Satellite Broadband Order, replacing the legacy EPFD framework with performance-based protection criteria. Up to 8 NGSO satellites can now serve the same geo/frequency band concurrently — a structural capacity unlock for Starlink, Kuiper, and follow-ons.
Why it matters
LEO competitive density just stepped up materially in the rural and exurban footprints where greenfield FTTH economics are most fragile.
Recommended action
Re-run your unserved/underserved take-rate model with LEO at 25%+ household penetration in any market with sub-15 homes-per-mile. If a market needs LEO under 15% to clear hurdle IRR, kill it from the build plan now and redeploy capital to denser passings.

XGS-PON is the 2026 default; 25G PON is a premium overlay; 50G PON pushed to 2028

🔴 Act Today 19/22
Tech & Strategy Available Now
What's emerging
XGS-PON (10G symmetric) is now the dominant new-build choice through 2030. 25GS-PON is gaining momentum but as a targeted overlay — premium residential, business, and select wholesale. 50G-PON adoption forecasts have slid from 2026 to 2028.
Why it matters for growth
Standardizing on XGS-PON now with 25G-capable optics in the OLT chassis preserves 10–15 years of upgrade headroom without paying the 25G premium today.
How to apply it
Lock all new builds to XGS-PON as default. Specify 25G-PON-capable OLT chassis on every greenfield purchase order so you can overlay 25G in business/MDU sub-rings without forklift upgrades. Skip 50G-PON entirely until 2027 RFI cycle.
