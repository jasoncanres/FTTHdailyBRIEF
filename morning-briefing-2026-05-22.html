Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

Why it matters: NEPA cluster (Wilkes-Barre, Scranton) is in scope. Subgrantees and tenant ISPs operating on Gigapower fiber gain a regulatory tailwind, and the open-access wholesale model is the cleanest play for multiple BEAD subgrantees inside one footprint.

FCC pole attachment rules effective May 8 — 30-day contractor approval, ISPs no longer bankroll pre-existing pole defects

20/22
Policy / Regulatory🟡 This Week

What happened: Updated FCC pole attachment rules took effect May 8. Utilities must approve new attacher-proposed contractors within 30 days or the request is deemed approved. The Comcast v. Appalachian Power ruling earlier this year bars pole owners from charging attachers to fix pre-existing safety violations as an access condition.

Why it matters: Aerial construction economics in PA and AL improve materially. Utility pushback is already organized, so the rules will be tested case-by-case — the operators who file disputes early define the precedents.

Action: Engineering and outside counsel build a one-page pole attachment escalation playbook by Friday May 29. Identify the first three NEPA poles where a pre-existing-defect charge has been quoted, and file an FCC informal complaint to test the new framework before utilities establish a workaround.

NTIA reaffirms BEAD compliance: no state side-deals on net neutrality or rate regulation

15/22
Policy / Regulatory🟢 Monitor

Why it matters: Tenant-ISP economics on the Gigapower platform stay intact in BEAD-funded territories. Confirms federal preemption of state-level wholesale rate pressure where BEAD money is in play.

Action: Wholesale BD updates tenant ISP master agreements to reference the NTIA guidance as a regulatory assumption. No external move required this week — queue for the next legal review cycle.

Supply Chain

Fiber supply collapse: AI data centers consume 36x more fiber per rack; Corning sold through 2026; preform capacity 18–24 months out

22/22
Supply Chain🔴 Act Today

What happened: Data center fiber demand grew 76% YoY in 2025 and is projected to hit 30% of global fiber demand by 2027. AI racks consume 36x more fiber than CPU racks. At least one major fiber manufacturer has sold all 2026 inventory. Lead times have stretched to 20 weeks for large buyers and a year for smaller ones. New preform capacity does not arrive until late 2026 / 2027.

Why it matters: Every Gigapower build in AZ, NV, PA, AL, and FL competes for the same fiber strands as a hyperscale data center buyer with deeper pockets and longer contracts. Construction schedules slip when crews run out of strand, not when permits clear.

Action: Engineering and Supply Chain submit a 12-month strand-volume forecast to Corning and Prysmian by next Friday (May 29) and convert open POs into firm-take contracts with allocation guarantees. Lock pricing through Q2 2027. Flag any market where reservation falls short of plan to the JV leadership before the June board meeting.
