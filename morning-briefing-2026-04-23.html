Section 1 — Intelligence Feed

Policy / Regulatory

BEAD at the finish line: 53 of 56 states approved, 44 signed — six-month ISP contracting window is running

18/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: NTIA has approved 53 of 56 Eligible Entity Final Proposals, with 51 NIST-cleared and 44 states having signed return award agreements. States now have a six-month ticking clock to finalize contracts with subgrantee ISPs.

Why it matters: Subgrantee selection, construction capacity, fiber allocation, and labor bookings all tighten from here. Operators that haven't locked contracts, permits, and supply are about to be squeezed by competitors that did.

Action: Pull your subgrantee contract status and construction-start timeline by state this week. Escalate any state where contracts aren't signed within 60 days of final approval.

NTIA pulls co-ops and munis into FCC pole attachment regime under BEAD — effective now

15/22
Policy / Regulatory🟡 This Week

What happened: Under BEAD rules, cooperatives and municipal utilities participating as subgrantees must now comply with FCC pole attachment rules. State broadband offices are required to enforce FCC attachment rules on poles not otherwise federally or state-regulated.

Why it matters: Pole owner intransigence has been the #1 time-killer on rural fiber builds. This materially shortens make-ready timelines and caps fees in BEAD-funded projects — a real cycle-time unlock in rural markets.

Action: Direct your OSP team to re-baseline make-ready schedules in co-op and muni-pole territories this week and flag any pole owner refusing the new BEAD standard for formal state broadband office dispute.

California turns on the nation's largest public broadband network — state-owned middle-mile now live

13/22
Policy / Regulatory🟢 Monitor

What happened: Governor Newsom announced California activated the first segment of its state-owned middle-mile broadband network on April 2, 2026, connecting the Bishop Paiute Tribe as the first rural community. The network is the largest publicly owned broadband infrastructure in the U.S.

Why it matters: Every state watching California will accelerate middle-mile public builds, pressuring wholesale transport pricing and giving smaller FTTH overbuilders a cheaper path into rural markets that were uneconomic on private backhaul.

Action: Have strategy pull the California middle-mile IRU pricing sheet when published and benchmark it against your current transport costs in comparable rural corridors.

Supply Chain

Corning fiber lead times blow out: 60+ weeks on ribbon, loose tube backordered into Q3 2026

20/22
Supply Chain🔴 Act Today

What happened: Corning's SVP and GM of Optical Communications confirmed ribbon fiber lead times are now 60+ weeks, with loose tube cable — the workhorse for outdoor FTTH — backordered into Q3 2026. Hyperscaler lockups, including the $6B Meta-Corning agreement, have consumed available 2026 capacity.

Why it matters: Any FTTH build planned for H2 2026 that doesn't already have fiber on a signed purchase order is at real risk of slipping 6–9 months. BEAD milestones, investor-committed homes passed, and competitive footprint races all depend on cable delivery you can hold in your hand.

Action: By Friday, cut a rolling 18-month fiber commit with Corning, Prysmian, or OFS locking SKU, quantities, and delivery windows. Treat fiber allocation as your #1 constraint and price it accordingly in build ROI models.

China germanium export curbs + Trump tariffs compressing fiber cost stack

15/22
Supply Chain🟡 This Week

What happened: China, source of ~80% of the germanium used in fiber glass preforms, has restricted exports, and Trump-era tariffs on imported telecom components remain active. Vendors cite both factors as structural pressure on 2026 cable pricing.

Why it matters: Expect mid-to-high-single-digit cable price increases in 2026 contracts. Operators that priced fiber builds on 2024 inputs will see margin compression on every mile deployed.

Action: Re-model FTTH unit economics with a 6–10% fiber cost increase by next week's operating review and force CFO conversation on whether pass-price or ARPU needs to move.

Competitive Intelligence

AT&T commits $250B, targets 40M fiber passings by year-end — pace accelerating to ~5M/year

18/22
Competitive Intelligence🔴 Act Today

What happened: AT&T announced a $250B, five-year capital commitment across fiber, wireless, and satellite. It will add 8M fiber locations in 2026 — 4M organic and 4M from the closed Lumen consumer asset purchase — pushing to 40M passings by year-end and 60M by decade's end.

Why it matters: AT&T and Verizon are building at a combined ~7M passings/year. For any mid-market operator not inside 3–5 years of an incumbent overbuild, the exit window on independent fiber plays is closing. Footprint locked now is worth more tomorrow.

Action: Map your top 20 MSA expansion candidates against AT&T and Verizon announced 2026–2027 build zones. Commit capital to markets where you arrive first, and re-evaluate any market where AT&T/Verizon ships within 18 months of your go-live.

Charter CEO says fiber overbuilds are slowing — capital discipline returning to the market

16/22
Competitive Intelligence🟡 This Week

What happened: Charter CEO Chris Winfrey publicly stated that competitive fiber overbuild activity has slowed as financing costs rose and operators reassessed ROI, noting the market is "waking up to the fact that much of it wasn't positive ROI to begin with." Charter itself is pushing its HFC upgrade completion into 2026.

Why it matters: Cap-light PE-backed overbuilders are the ones pulling back — not AT&T or Verizon. That means less subscale overbuild noise in your markets and a real M&A opening to pick up distressed or unfinished fiber footprints at better multiples.

Action: Have corp dev generate a target list by Friday of PE-backed overbuilders with paused builds or covenant pressure within 250 miles of your existing footprint. Open conversations with two of them this month.

Verizon-Frontier integration complete: 30M passings, >1M annual build cadence

17/22
Competitive Intelligence🔴 Act Today

What happened: Verizon closed the $20B Frontier acquisition in Q1 2026 and integrated operations under "Frontier, a Verizon Company." Combined footprint is ~30M passings across 31 states plus DC, with combined annual build cadence now exceeding 1M passings per year and a 40–50M long-term target.

Why it matters: Verizon is now a credible coast-to-coast FTTH peer to AT&T. Every Frontier legacy market will see ARPU, speed tier, and bundling repositioned this year — your competitive assumptions in overlapping markets are now stale.

Action: Refresh competitive teardowns on every Frontier/Verizon overlap market by May 15 — pricing, speed tiers, mobile bundle discounts, and churn triggers — and set a Q2 pricing response.

Market Trends

U.S. fiber passes 58% of households — cable tipping into second place in 2026

14/22
Market Trends🟡 This Week

What happened: FTTH now passes 58% of U.S. households, up 13 points YoY. Analysts expect cable to lose #1 access-technology share to fiber inside 2026, mirroring a crossover Canada hit in 2025.

Why it matters: The narrative flip — "fiber is now the default" — unlocks MDU bulk wins, homebuilder exclusives, and wholesale conversations that were friction-heavy a year ago. Sales motion should lean into "you're the last cable holdout on your block."

Action: Direct sales to rebuild the MDU and new-construction pitch deck around the cable-to-fiber crossover by next Monday — lead with "fiber is the majority" framing, not "fiber is the upgrade."

Starlink at ~3M U.S. subs; FWA covers 60% of residential — the rural competitive set is no longer just cable

13/22
Market Trends🟢 Monitor

What happened: Starlink ended 2025 with ~3M U.S. subscribers and is now offered to effectively 100% of U.S. addresses. Fixed wireless from AT&T, T-Mobile, and Verizon climbed from 44% to 60% residential coverage between 2022 and 2025.

Why it matters: In rural FTTH builds, your real churn risk isn't cable — it's Starlink and FWA sitting at $50–70/month with no install friction. Take-rate models built on "fiber is the only alternative" overstate penetration.

Action: Cut take-rate assumptions in rural BEAD markets by 5–8 points in the next financial model refresh and validate against actual 12-month take-rate data from your most-mature rural buildout.

GFiber + Astound merger — Stonepeak takes control, Alphabet keeps minority, Q4 2026 close

17/22
M&A🔴 Act Today

What happened: Alphabet agreed to spin GFiber into a merger with Stonepeak-owned Astound Broadband. GFiber contributes ~2.8M fiber passings across 15 states; Astound adds ~4.5M passings across 13 states. Stonepeak holds the majority; Alphabet retains a minority stake. Expected close Q4 2026.

Section 3 — Technology & Growth Strategy

AI data centers reshaping the fiber market: +213M miles needed by 2029, 10–20x fiber per rack

17/22
Technology🔴 Act Today

What happened: Analysts now project the U.S. needs to add 213M fiber miles by 2029, more than doubling today's ~160M. Blackwell-class AI racks use 16x the fiber of traditional cloud racks. Ciena raised FY26 revenue guidance to $5.7–6.1B on AI demand; Nokia committed $4B to U.S. AI-ready network R&D and manufacturing.

Why it matters: FTTH operators are no longer just residential ISPs — dark fiber, wave, and on-ramp capacity to hyperscale campuses is the highest-ARPU wholesale product in the market. Any metro within 50 miles of a planned AI data center is now a wholesale revenue opportunity.

Action: Commission a data-center-adjacency map of your existing fiber plant within 30 days and have sales stand up a wholesale/hyperscaler product line by Q3.

XGS-PON is now the default; 25G PON ramping for operators with headroom ambitions

13/22
Technology🟡 This Week

What happened: Nokia launched a 16-port high-density 25G PON line card supporting GPON/XGS-PON/25G PON concurrently; Calix has active 25G trials with ALLO and Brightspeed; Adtran/Netomnia announced the first U.K. commercial 50G PON deployment. XGS-PON is now the mainstream residential PON standard.

Why it matters: A residential-only operator building new plant today in GPON is building a legacy network. XGS-PON is table stakes; 25G PON on the same OLT line card futureproofs the enterprise/wholesale upside.

Action: Mandate XGS-PON minimum for all new OLT deployments and require any new platform RFP to include a validated 25G PON upgrade path on the same chassis.

ARPU and churn dynamics flipping: price locks beat promo churn in 2026

13/22
Technology🟡 This Week

What happened: Spectrum, Verizon, and Xfinity have normalized no-contract price locks, trading ARPU ceiling for churn reduction and trust. Analysts expect cable broadband ARPU to decline in 2026 as fiber gains share, while fiber-driven churn benefits become more visible for telcos that lead with transparent pricing.

Why it matters: Promo/rate-shock churn is now the biggest lifetime-value leak in fiber. Price-lock models with auto-renew at current rate outperform traditional 12-month promos in LTV, even at lower initial ARPU.

Action: Pilot a 36-month price-lock SKU in one market this quarter; measure 90-day and 180-day take-rate and churn vs. control cell before rolling out broadly.
