What Happened

The Fiber Broadband Association confirmed this week that distribution through state broadband offices is occurring in Q1 2026, with construction ramp expected in H2 2026. 2Verizon-Frontier Integration Live — 30M Passings, SE Markets Reshaping

Signal Strength
What Happened

Verizon closed its $20B acquisition of Frontier Communications on January 20, 2026, immediately gaining ~30 million fiber passings across 31 states. The combined entity has committed to 1 million+ new fiber passings per year and Frontier's pre-close commitment of 2.8 million additional locations by end of 2026 remains in the deployment plan. Integration is active — brand consolidation underway, and Verizon has confirmed it is targeting 35–40 million total passings as the long-term build goal.

Why It Matters

In any market where Frontier previously operated, you now have a better-capitalized, 5G-bundling tier-1 carrier deploying fiber at institutional scale — the competitive window in those footprints is closing fast.

Recommended Action

Run a Frontier/Verizon footprint overlap against your current and pipeline markets this week. Any market where Frontier had copper but hadn't begun fiber conversion is now a priority target — Verizon will get there, but integration lag is your 12–18 month window. Accelerate permitting and pre-sell in those corridors before Verizon's 1M/yr pace reaches them.

3BEAD's $21B Surplus + Tech-Neutrality Shift Creates Competitive Risk for Fiber Operators
21 / 22 🔴 Act Today
Signal Strength
What Happened

NTIA has confirmed that states collectively came in $21 billion under initial BEAD budget projections and has signaled guidance on reallocation in early 2026 — that guidance is now overdue and expected imminently. Simultaneously, the June 2025 BEAD Restructuring Policy Notice established technology neutrality, meaning Fixed Wireless Access (FWA) and Low Earth Orbit satellite providers (SpaceX Starlink et al.) are now eligible to compete for the same funding pools previously reserved for fiber. NTIA also rescinded all non-deployment spending approvals, including digital equity funds.

Why It Matters

$21B of reallocation funds will be contested by FWA and LEO operators who have lower capital requirements — fiber operators must make the cost-per-passing and performance case explicitly to state offices, or risk losing reallocation awards to faster-to-deploy alternatives.

Recommended Action

Prepare a concise fiber-vs-FWA-vs-LEO cost/performance one-pager benchmarked to NTIA scoring criteria and get it in front of your target state broadband directors before the reallocation guidance drops. Frame it around long-term performance thresholds and total lifecycle cost — the metrics where fiber wins. Do not wait for the guidance to be published to start this conversation.
