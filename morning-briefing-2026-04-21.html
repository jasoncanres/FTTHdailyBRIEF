FCC Order Eliminates Network-Change Filing Requirements for Copper Retirement

21/22🔴 Act Today
[Policy/Regulatory]

What happened: The FCC published "Reducing Barriers to Network Improvements and Service Changes" in the Federal Register on April 20. The order eliminates all filing requirements in the Commission's network-change disclosure rules and removes the public-notice step for short-term changes and copper retirements — explicitly designed to redirect capital from legacy copper maintenance to fiber builds.

Why it matters: ILEC overbuild windows are shortening. Incumbents can retire copper faster and pivot capex to fiber; greenfield operators lose a timing arbitrage they've relied on.

Recommended action: Pull the list of ILEC copper assets in your targeted CBSAs and model a 12-month overbuild advance. Any market where copper retirement is plausible inside 18 months — accelerate the build or cede it.

House Omnibus Broadband Permitting Bill Reaches Rules Committee

21/22🔴 Act Today
[Policy/Regulatory]

What happened: On April 20, the House Rules Committee took up a ~100-page omnibus bill consolidating 20+ permitting and preemption provisions across wireless siting, wireline deployment, cable franchising, and federal environmental review. The bill explicitly constrains local government authority to delay or add cost to tower and fiber deployments.

Why it matters: If enacted, the economics of small-market fiber flip — permit drag has been one of the top three gating factors on rural FTTH IRR.

Recommended action: Draft a one-page "Upside Case" model that reprices your expansion pipeline assuming a 30% reduction in permitting cycle time. Share with your board before the May finance meeting.

NetCo/ServCo Open-Access Separation Gaining Traction Early Deployment

18/22🟡 This Week
[Tech & Growth]

What's emerging: PE sponsors are increasingly separating network assets (NetCo) from retail service (ServCo) to compress capital intensity and allow multiple retailers on a single fiber. Nokia is formally pitching this as the preferred MDU deployment model.

Why it matters for growth: Open-access converts a unit-economics headwind into a multi-tenant wholesale revenue layer — a meaningful IRR lift in any market where retail penetration caps below 45%.

How to apply it: Pilot test — select one SC market with subscale take-rate projections, structure it as open access with a wholesale SLA. Model the IRR delta vs. single-retailer base case before the next board meeting. Why: de-risks marginal markets without requiring capital increase.
