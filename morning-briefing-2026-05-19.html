FCC pole attachment rules took effect May 8 — new utility timelines and streamlined contractor approvals

20/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: The Wireline Competition Bureau's May 7 public notice set May 8, 2026 as the effective date for the new Section 224 pole attachment rules. The order tightens utility response timelines and pre-approves qualified contractors to perform make-ready, two of the longest-cycle items in any aerial fiber build.

Why it matters: Gigapower's NE PA cluster (Wilkes-Barre/Scranton) and any future AL/FL aerial work just gained federal leverage against ILEC and electric pole owners — but only if Engineering invokes the new timelines correctly on every attachment request.

Action: Engineering and Outside Counsel — issue a standing memo by Friday updating Gigapower's pole attachment request template and tracker to cite the May 8 rules, the new utility response clock, and the contractor pre-approval pathway.

Eighth Circuit vacates FCC Digital Discrimination Rule in its entirety

18/22
Policy / Regulatory🟡 This Week

What happened: A unanimous Eighth Circuit panel held the FCC exceeded its Section 60506 authority and vacated the 2023 rule, including its disparate-impact framework and its reach to entities tangentially involved in broadband. There is currently no operative federal disparate-impact regime for broadband deployment.

Why it matters: Compliance overhead and litigation risk on Gigapower's tenant-ISP selection and footprint sequencing decisions drops materially. The risk shifts to state-level analogues that may try to fill the gap.

Action: JV/Parent liaison — confirm AT&T and BlackRock Legal have updated the Gigapower compliance playbook to reflect the vacatur, and ask Government Affairs to scan AZ, NV, PA, AL, FL legislatures for state-level disparate-impact bills by end of week.
