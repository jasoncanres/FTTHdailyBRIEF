Policy / Regulatory

FCC pole attachment reform takes effect May 8 — 30-day contractor approval, 120-day window for 3,000–6,000 pole orders

22/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: Amendments to 47 CFR §§1.1403, 1.1411, 1.1412 became effective May 7–8, 2026. Utilities must respond within 30 days to contractor approval requests or the contractor is deemed approved. A new 120-day make-ready window applies to attachment orders of 3,000–6,000 poles. Attachers must give 60-day pre-submission notice for orders over 300 poles or forfeit FCC timeline rights. BEAD subgrantee co-ops and munis now fall inside the FCC pole regime.

Why it matters: Compresses the critical path on every Gigapower aerial mile and converts a historically open-ended utility negotiation into a hard clock — but only for filings structured to claim the rule's protections.

Action: Engineering and Permitting jointly publish, by Friday May 22, a revised pole-attachment playbook for Mesa/Chandler/Gilbert (SRP, APS) and the NEPA cluster (PPL, UGI) that triggers the 60-day pre-submission notice for every queued order over 300 poles. Government Affairs files a Gigapower-specific contractor list with each pole owner this week to start the 30-day clock.

Nevada BEAD final approval drops fiber share from 80% to 64%, lifts LEO satellite to 29% of unserved locations

22/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: NTIA approved Nevada's revised final proposal unlocking $170M of the state's $416M BEAD allocation. Under the Trump administration's tech-neutral rewrite, fiber now serves 64% of unserved Nevada locations (down from 80%); LEO satellite captures 29%. Phase III subgrantee selections are public; deployment begins mid-2026.

Why it matters: Nevada is Gigapower's flagship and the take-rate proof point for the JV. Loss of 16 points of fiber share means tenant ISPs face a permanent satellite floor on rural pricing, and the addressable pool for AT&T Fiber/Flume/Dojo in greater Clark County just contracted at the margin.

Action: Wholesale BD walks every BEAD subgrantee fiber awardee in Clark and Washoe counties this week to test wholesale carriage on Gigapower middle mile. Government Affairs confirms with the Nevada Broadband Office whether Gigapower-served census blocks remain BEAD-eligible for tenant ISP rural extensions.

Supply Chain

Fiber lead times blow past 60+ weeks for ribbon, into Q3 2026 for loose tube; replacement quotes up 70–80%

22/22
Supply Chain🔴 Act Today

What happened: Only three BABA-compliant U.S. glass suppliers (Corning, OFS, Prysmian). Ribbon fiber sits at 60+ weeks; loose tube — the primary FTTH cable — has slipped into Q3 2026 availability. ISPs that lost their original allocations are getting replacement quotes 70–80% above prior pricing. Corning's $6B Meta deal (announced January 2026) and AI data center demand at 36x per-rack consumption are absorbing G.657A capacity. China's germanium export controls add upstream risk.

Why it matters: Supply is now the binding constraint on Gigapower's 1.5M-passings target. Every quarter of waited contracting raises cost basis by 15–25 points and concedes shovel windows to competitors who have already locked allocations.

Action: Procurement and Engineering co-sign 24-month forward allocations with two of the three BABA-compliant glass suppliers by Friday May 22, indexed to AZ/NV/PA pass plans. Lock loose tube cable at Q3 2026 vintage pricing this week — before Fiber Connect (May 17–20) closes the negotiating window.

AI data center fiber demand projected at 214M new fiber miles by 2029, displacing FTTH allocation at the mill

18/22
Supply Chain🟡 This Week

What happened: AI-dense racks consume 36x the fiber of legacy CPU racks. Cumulative hyperscale capacity is forecast to triple within several years. Manufacturers have shifted G.652D telecom output toward G.657A AI/data-center demand, creating secondary shortages in standard FTTH cable. Global data center fiber demand grew 75.9% YoY in 2025; projected 30% of all fiber demand by 2027 (vs 5% in 2024).

Why it matters: Hyperscale fiber demand is structurally priced higher than FTTH at the mill, so Gigapower competes for the same glass as Meta and Microsoft on inferior unit economics — unless contracting moves to multi-year locked-volume terms.

Action: Procurement adds a "hyperscale displacement clause" — minimum quarterly allocation, no force-majeure carve-out for hyperscale orders — to every cable contract renewal this quarter.

Section 3 — Technology & Growth Strategy

Adtran + Netomnia stand up first commercial 50G PON; Nokia ships line card supporting GPON/XGS-PON/25G/50G concurrently

15/22
Technology🟡 This Week

What happened: Adtran and U.K. operator Netomnia announced the first commercial 50G PON deployment in May 2026. Nokia released a 16-port high-density line card supporting GPON, XGS-PON, 25G PON, and 50G PON on the same hardware, allowing operators to run residential XGS-PON and enterprise 25G/50G simultaneously off one OLT chassis.

Why it matters: Gigapower's current XGS-PON architecture has a clear upgrade path on commodity hardware. Tenant ISP differentiation will start to flow from PON tier, not just speed.

Action: Engineering opens a 30-day evaluation of Nokia's concurrent multi-rate line card against Gigapower's current OLT roadmap. Wholesale BD scopes a 25G PON wholesale enterprise tier as a new SKU for the data-center-adjacent footprint in Mesa, Las Vegas, and Albuquerque.
