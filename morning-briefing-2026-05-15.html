Policy / Regulatory

FCC pole attachment rules took effect May 8 — new timelines, contractor approvals, accelerated dispute docket

22/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: Updated FCC pole attachment rules — approved unanimously in July 2025 — went live May 8, 2026. Rules set firm timelines for utility processing of large pole attachment requests, expand the use of qualified contractors for surveys and make-ready, and operationalize the Rapid Broadband Assessment Team accelerated docket (already used by Comcast vs. APCO in February 2026).

Why it matters: Gigapower's PA aerial construction sits in the most pole-dependent part of the JV footprint. The new contractor and timeline regime collapses make-ready cycle time and creates an enforceable backstop where pole owners stall.

Action: Engineering and Government Affairs convene Monday May 18 to inventory every pending pole application in Luzerne and Lackawanna counties, rebaseline timelines against the May 8 rules, and pre-stage qualified contractors before Greenlight files competing make-ready requests.

Alabama awards $460M BEAD across 63 projects — AT&T/BellSouth wins $93M for fiber, Comcast wins $157M

22/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: Governor Ivey announced final BEAD subgrants in late March / April 2026: $460M across 63 projects covering ~92,000 unserved locations. 71% of projects are fiber, 24% LEO, 5% HFC. AT&T's BellSouth entity captured $93.2M; Comcast captured $157.2M.

Why it matters: AT&T-funded fiber going into out-of-footprint AL territory is a JV adjacency question. Comcast's $157M creates a coax-to-fiber competitor in any community where Gigapower targets wholesale build. Project-level service area maps now drive AL market selection for the JV.

Action: Wholesale BD and JV/Parent liaison pull the ADECA project-level award map by May 22, overlay against the AL target list, and identify communities where the AT&T BEAD award creates federal-funding overlap risk versus Gigapower greenfield economics.

Nevada BEAD plan rescinded fiber-only mandate — final $416M plan now mixes fiber, LEO, FWA

21/22
Policy / Regulatory🟡 This Week

What happened: Nevada's BEAD final plan, approved January 2026 after Trump administration revisions, unlocked $170M of the state's $416M allocation. Fiber-only prioritization was struck; the plan now allows technology-neutral subgrants including LEO and fixed wireless. BEAD deployment starts mid-2026.

Why it matters: Federally-subsidized LEO/FWA in Clark County perimeter zones erodes the case for greenfield FTTH outside the dense Vegas core. Wholesale economics weaken in any submarket where a subsidized LEO competitor captures the unserved layer.

Action: Government Affairs request Nevada Broadband Office subgrantee selection criteria by May 22. Wholesale BD reassess Clark County expansion zones — promote tier 1 dense submarkets, demote tier 3 unserved-edge buildouts where subsidized LEO will price the floor.

NTIA BEAD update extends FCC pole rules to co-op and municipal poles — January 2026 terms force compliance

20/22
Policy / Regulatory🟡 This Week

What happened: NTIA's January 2026 BEAD general terms and conditions update requires co-ops and municipal utilities accepting BEAD subgrants to comply with FCC pole attachment rate caps, timelines, and contractor provisions — historically exempt under Section 224.

Why it matters: AL and FL have heavy electric co-op pole ownership. PA has muni-owned utility poles in several Lackawanna and Luzerne boroughs. Co-op poles that previously priced and timed at owner discretion now have a federal compliance hook where any BEAD-funded attacher is present.

Action: Government Affairs map every co-op and muni pole owner across AL, FL, and PA target markets by May 29. Flag which received BEAD subgrants. Letter the BEAD ones requesting Section 224-equivalent rate schedules and timelines before construction season ramps.

18/22
Supply Chain🟡 This Week

What happened: Industry analysis projects U.S. fiber route miles must roughly double — from 159.6M to 372.9M — by 2029 to meet AI data center demand, with AI-optimized racks consuming 2–10x the fiber of traditional CPU configurations. Why it matters: FTTH buildouts compete for the same Corning, Prysmian, OFS allocation that hyperscalers are now locking up multi-year. Lead times and price for BABA-compliant glass will worsen through 2026. Three U.S. BABA-compliant manufacturers means no spot relief.

Action: Construction and Procurement lock 2027 cable allocations with Corning and Prysmian by June 30 at current pricing. Issue a written notice to Engineering that aerial/duct design must assume +15% glass cost and +6 week lead time for any Q1 2027 build.
