Policy / Regulatory

Arizona Permit Finder Phase 2 — design-upload permit engine — lands by year-end across Maricopa

21/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: Arizona's State Broadband Office is building Phase 2 of its first-in-the-nation Permit Finder, launching by end of 2026. Providers will upload network designs and the tool returns the exact permit set and administering agencies required — covering Maricopa County municipalities including Mesa, Chandler, and Gilbert. Phase 1 already consolidates permit requirements and agency points-of-contact statewide.

Why it matters: This directly compresses permitting timelines in Gigapower's three active AZ construction markets and standardizes the ROW data Permitting needs per municipality.

Action: Permitting — register on the AZ Permit Finder this week, map Mesa/Chandler/Gilbert permit sets against current dossiers, and email beadpermitting@azcommerce.com to confirm Phase 2 beta access for design uploads.

BEAD pivots technology-neutral; $21B in non-deployment funds still frozen with no final guidance

18/22
Policy / Regulatory🟡 This Week

What happened: All 56 states/territories have final proposals submitted; 54 approved by NTIA, 52 with funds released (dashboard updated June 8). Lutnick's revised guidance shifted BEAD away from a fiber preference toward technology-neutral awards — draft plans now show ~66% fiber, ~21% LEO satellite, ~11% fixed wireless. The promised early-2026 guidance on $21B in "non-deployment" savings has not been finalized.

Why it matters: Technology-neutral scoring lets fixed wireless and LEO undercut fiber in marginal census blocks adjacent to Gigapower's greenfield builds, reshaping where wholesale demand concentrates.

Action: Government Affairs — pull the approved AZ and NV final proposals by Friday, flag any fiber-eligible blocks reallocated to FWA/LEO inside or bordering Gigapower footprint, and brief Wholesale BD on take-rate exposure.

Supply Chain

Fiber lead times stretch to a full year for smaller buyers as AI data centers consume 36x more glass

21/22
Supply Chain🔴 Act Today

What happened: The optical fiber supply chain is in its tightest squeeze in memory — driven by AI buildouts, a glass-preform bottleneck, and defense demand. Lead times are 20 weeks for large-volume buyers and up to a year for smaller purchasers. Corning has locked multibillion-dollar multiyear deals with Amazon (June 8), Meta (up to $6B), and a $300M Nvidia investment for new NC/TX plants. Data center fiber demand grew ~76% YoY in 2025 and heads toward ~30% of global demand by 2027.

Why it matters: Gigapower's build cadence depends on cable allocation it does not currently have priced against hyperscaler-scale contracts; a year-long lead time silently slips every greenfield schedule.

Action: Engineering + Procurement — convene a fiber-allocation review with Corning/Prysmian/OFS this week to lock 2026–2027 cable volumes against the AZ/NV/PA build plan before hyperscaler demand prices out the merchant market.

Competitive Intelligence

AT&T unveils $250B plan for 5G, fiber, and satellite; targets 60M fiber passings by 2030

18/22
Parent / JV Watch🟡 This Week

What happened: JV parent and anchor tenant AT&T announced a $250B multiyear infrastructure investment spanning 5G, fiber, and satellite. It is lifting fiber build pace from 3M passings in 2025 to a ~4M run rate by year-end 2026, targeting 40M passings this year en route to 60M by 2030, with the $5.75B Lumen consumer-fiber acquisition adding ~4M locations.

Why it matters: AT&T's in-footprint fiber acceleration sets the capital and homes-passed backdrop the JV's incremental builds are measured against, and signals continued anchor-tenant demand for Gigapower capacity out-of-footprint.

Action: JV/Parent liaison — confirm with AT&T whether the $250B envelope changes Gigapower's incremental-to-AT&T build mandate or the 1.5M-location target, and report back to Wholesale BD before next JV governance review.

Fidium leans on FTTH operating history to build out a wholesale fiber strategy

15/22
Competitive & Wholesale Peer🟢 Monitor

What happened: Fidium (Consolidated) is reframing its retail FTTH experience as the foundation for a wholesale fiber offer, joining Metronet's shift to a wholesale ISP model under T-Mobile. The open-access/wholesale posture is moving from niche to mainstream among Tier 2 operators.

Why it matters: More wholesale peers validates Gigapower's open-access thesis but also seeds future competition for the same tenant ISPs and infrastructure-fund capital.

Action: Wholesale BD — add Fidium and Metronet wholesale terms to the competitive matrix this week; benchmark their tenant pricing and SLA structure against Gigapower's open-access rate card.

Market Trends

66% of fiber operators are slowing or stopping builds; 73% expect to do M&A within a year

16/22
Market Trends🟢 Monitor

What happened: Analyst surveys show 66% of fiber operators slowing or halting new builds to protect unit economics and 73% likely to engage in M&A over the next year. Sub-300K-location operators are rolling up sub-50K mom-and-pops. US fiber availability now reaches ~60% of households, up 11% in 2025, but the market is shifting from land-grab to capital discipline.

Why it matters: A pullback in retail builds widens the white space where a wholesale open-access platform can aggregate demand from multiple ISPs instead of competing build-for-build.

Action: Wholesale BD — identify stalled sub-scale builders inside the NV/AZ/PA footprint as candidate tenant ISPs or fiber-acquisition targets; draft a one-page open-access pitch for operators capitulating on capex.

18/22
M&A Watch🟡 This Week

Section 3 — Technology & Growth Strategy

AI-driven fiber demand reshapes PON roadmap as vendors split on 25G vs 50G next-gen path

16/22
Technology & Growth🟢 Monitor

What happened: Access vendors are repositioning for AI-fueled fiber demand. Nokia, DZS and Ciena are pushing 25G PON now (Nokia's Quillion line cards run GPON/XGS-PON/25G concurrently), while Adtran and Calix argue 50G PON will be the more cost-effective leap once XGS-PON saturates. The AI buildout's 36x-per-rack fiber intensity is pulling roadmap and capacity decisions forward.

Why it matters: Gigapower's OLT/ONT platform choice today determines whether the network can serve high-capacity business and data-center tenants without a forklift upgrade — a wholesale differentiator.

Action: Engineering — validate that the current OLT platform supports a non-disruptive XGS-PON-to-25G path; request 25G vs 50G roadmaps from Nokia and Calix this quarter to inform the next capex cycle.
