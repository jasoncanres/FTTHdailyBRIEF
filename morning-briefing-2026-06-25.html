Section 1 — Intelligence Feed

Policy / Regulatory

FCC opens inquiry into certified-state pole-attachment regimes; comment window closes July 13

20/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: The FCC Wireline Competition Bureau issued a June 2026 Public Notice reminding "reverse-preempted" states of their duty to actively regulate pole attachment rates, terms, and complaint timelines, and seeking comment on whether certification rules need tightening to stop deployment delays. It follows the Bureau's June 11 "first-of-its-kind" order resolving a Comcast pole dispute in 60 days to protect BEAD builds. Comments are due July 13, reply comments July 27.

Why it matters: Pennsylvania and Florida pole-attachment friction is the single largest schedule risk to Gigapower aerial builds in the NE PA (Wilkes-Barre/Scranton) cluster, and a faster federal make-ready clock directly compresses time-to-revenue.

Action: Direct Government Affairs to file a short comment by July 13 documenting any PA/FL make-ready delays Gigapower has hit, and confirm with Engineering which footprint states are reverse-preempted vs. FCC-jurisdictional before the next aerial permit package.

54 states clear BEAD Final Proposals; Arizona and Pennsylvania approved as build phase opens 2026–2030

18/22
Policy / Regulatory🟡 This Week

What happened: NTIA reports 54 states and territories have approved Final Proposals, 52 with grant funds available and 52 with signed award agreements. Arizona and Pennsylvania — both Gigapower footprint states — are approved, with the bulk of construction running 2026 through 2030. Fiber still represents 80%+ of planned builds despite the dropped "fiber-first" mandate.

Why it matters: BEAD subgrantee builds in AZ and PA will compete head-to-head with Gigapower for splicers, boring crews, and conduit in the exact metros where the JV is permitting — a labor and supply squeeze, not a subsidy opportunity, given the wholesale model.

Action: Have Construction pull the AZ and PA BEAD subgrantee award maps this week and overlay them on Gigapower's Maricopa and Luzerne/Lackawanna build plans to flag crew-contention zones before Q3 mobilization.

Supply Chain

Ribbon fiber lead times blow past 60 weeks; FBA calls BEAD-plus-hyperscaler demand "the perfect storm"

20/22
Supply Chain🔴 Act Today

What happened: Superior Essex puts ribbon fiber lead times in the "60-plus weeks" range and loose-tube well into Q3 2026. The FBA Supply Chain Working Group chair calls it "the perfect storm" — BEAD states mobilizing simultaneously while hyperscalers absorb glass and pull crews to data-center projects. At least one major manufacturer is sold out through 2026.

Why it matters: A 60-week ribbon lead time means cable for any Gigapower build not already on PO is a 2027 delivery — this dictates whether the next NV/AZ/PA phases hit their pass-count targets on schedule.

Action: Engineering and Procurement should lock 2026–2027 fiber and OLT/ONT allocations against committed PO volume this week, and pressure-test every unbuilt phase's schedule against a 60-week glass assumption before committing pass-count dates to the JV board.

Corning locks multibillion-dollar Amazon and NVIDIA fiber deals, deepening data-center crowd-out of FTTH glass

18/22
Supply Chain🟡 This Week

What happened: Corning signed a multibillion-dollar, multiyear optical-fiber supply agreement with Amazon (announced June 8) adding 1,000 NC manufacturing jobs, on top of a May NVIDIA deal to expand US fiber capacity by 50%+ and connectivity capacity 10x. CEO Wendell Weeks confirmed one major customer's fiber inventory is effectively sold out through 2026.

Why it matters: Hyperscaler off-take is consuming the same capacity expansion that FTTH builders are counting on, meaning Gigapower's glass pricing and availability are now set by AI data-center demand, not broadband demand.

Action: Procurement should secure a written multiyear allocation commitment from Gigapower's primary cable supplier now rather than buying on spot, and brief the JV/Parent liaison that AT&T's scale may be the lever to guarantee priority allocation.

Competitive & Wholesale Peer Intelligence

T-Mobile turns Lumos and Metronet into anchor-tenant open-access networks, targeting 12–15M fiber locations

21/22
Competitive / Peer🟡 This Week

What happened: With the Lumos deal closed in April and Metronet folded in via the KKR JV, T-Mobile now operates Lumos and Metronet as open-access networks with T-Mobile as anchor tenant and other ISPs invited to wholesale — the same structural model as Gigapower. T-Mobile has fiber to 500,000+ households across 10 states and is targeting 12–15 million locations.

Why it matters: T-Mobile is now a direct wholesale open-access peer at national scale, which validates Gigapower's model but also creates a competing tenant-ISP marketplace that could pull the same regional and MDU ISPs Gigapower needs as tenants.

Action: Wholesale BD should benchmark T-Mobile's published tenant-ISP terms against Gigapower's wholesale rate card this week and identify any MDU/regional ISP already on Lumos or Metronet that overlaps a Gigapower footprint metro.

AT&T posts record 584K Q1 broadband adds, hits 42% convergence rate counting Gigapower toward 60M+ passings by 2030

21/22
Parent / JV Watch🟡 This Week

What happened: AT&T's Q1 2026 results show a record 584,000 fiber and fixed-wireless adds and 5.7 million converged customers (42% convergence rate), a figure that explicitly blends AT&T owned-and-operated fiber with the Gigapower JV. Management reiterated a target of 60M+ fiber passings by 2030, counting Gigapower, the Lumen Mass Markets assets, and other open-access agreements. The convergence story drove AT&T's stock narrative as recently as June 23.

Why it matters: Gigapower is now an explicit line item in AT&T's 60M-passing convergence math, which means the JV's pass-count delivery is being measured against parent commitments to investors — raising the cost of any schedule slip.

Action: JV/Parent liaison should confirm with AT&T's fiber planning team how many of the 60M passings AT&T is attributing to Gigapower by year, so Construction's build plan is reconciled to the number AT&T has already given the Street.

Market Trends

AI data centers consume 36x the fiber of standard racks; segment to hit 30% of global fiber demand by 2027

16/22
Market Trends🟡 This Week

What happened: FBA and OFC 2026 coverage quantify the AI multiplier: GPU-dense data centers require roughly 36x more fiber than CPU racks, data-center fiber demand grew ~76% year-on-year in 2025, and the segment is projected to reach 30% of total global fiber demand by 2027.

Why it matters: This is the structural driver behind every FTTH supply constraint above — it is not a temporary spike, so Gigapower should plan multiyear procurement around a permanently tighter glass market.

Action: Wholesale BD should evaluate whether any Gigapower footprint metro sits near announced AI data-center builds, where the JV could wholesale capacity to hyperscaler middle-mile demand as an incremental revenue line.

Section 3 — Technology & Growth Strategy

Nokia ships high-density 25G PON line card on Quillion chipset supporting GPON, XGS, and 25G on one card

15/22
Technology🟢 Monitor

What happened: Nokia launched a high-density 25G PON line card built on its Quillion chipset that runs GPON, XGS-PON, and 25G PON simultaneously, positioning a software-driven upgrade path from today's XGS-PON installs. Adtran, by contrast, is arguing 50G PON will ultimately be the more cost-effective end state.

Why it matters: Multi-PON line cards let an open-access operator offer differentiated speed tiers to multiple tenant ISPs on shared OLT hardware without forklift upgrades — a wholesale economics advantage worth specifying into Gigapower's platform roadmap.

Action: Engineering should confirm whether Gigapower's deployed OLT platform supports a card-level 25G PON upgrade path and add a multi-PON tenant-tiering requirement to the next platform RFP.

Lock 2026–2027 fiber and OLT allocation against committed POs this week, using AT&T's scale as the lever. The convergence of 60-plus-week ribbon lead times, a Corning customer sold out through 2026, and hyperscaler off-take that now sets the price of glass means Gigapower's pass-count schedule is constrained by supply, not demand or permitting.
