Policy / Regulatory

FCC pole-attachment rules took effect May 8 — make-ready timelines, contractor self-help, and rate caps now enforceable across every Gigapower market

22/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: Updated federal pole-attachment rules adopted by the FCC last July became enforceable May 8, 2026. The rules tighten utility response timelines on attachment requests, codify contractor self-help where the pole owner misses deadlines, and reaffirm Comcast v. Appalachian Power: new attachers pay only the incremental cost of their own equipment, not preexisting safety violations.

Why it matters: Pole make-ready is the single largest controllable cost and schedule risk in aerial Gigapower builds across PA, AL, FL, and rural-edge AZ/NV — and pole owners will not volunteer concessions.

Action: Government Affairs and Engineering co-issue, within 7 days, an audit of every open pole-make-ready quote across the five-state footprint and formally invoke the new contractor self-help and rate-cap provisions on any quote that does not comply.

22/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: NTIA approved the Pennsylvania Broadband Development Authority's BEAD Final Proposal February 9, 2026. Pennsylvania holds $1.16B of allocation, with Appalachian/rural corridors prioritized. Subgrant award rounds are advancing through 2026.

Why it matters: NE PA is a Gigapower Intelligence-stage cluster. BEAD subgrantees will lock multi-year build commitments around Wilkes-Barre and Scranton; passing on coordination now means either competing against subsidized fiber on the rural edge or watching a competitor stitch the metro fringe shut.

How it differs: Gigapower's wholesale-only posture is BEAD-compatible as either subgrantee or open-access transport partner to retail ISP subgrantees — a position no incumbent ILEC in PA can match cleanly.

Action: Government Affairs to formally engage PBDA and request the active Luzerne/Lackawanna subgrant award list by Friday; Wholesale BD to package an open-access transport offer for the three nearest subgrantees within 14 days.

NTIA April rule extends FCC pole-attachment regime to co-op and municipal poles for BEAD subgrantees — opens a leverage door on previously unregulated pole owners

19/22
Policy / Regulatory🟡 This Week

What happened: NTIA's April 2026 BEAD guidance requires state broadband offices to compel BEAD-subgrantee pole owners — including electric co-ops and municipally-owned utilities — to comply with FCC rate caps, attachment timelines, and self-help provisions. Historically, Section 224 only reached IOU and ILEC poles.

Why it matters: A meaningful share of pole inventory in NE PA, rural AL, and parts of FL is co-op or municipal. Where those owners take BEAD money, Gigapower (as a third-party attacher in adjacent territory) now has an indirect lever to challenge non-conforming rates and timelines.

Action: Legal and Government Affairs map every co-op/muni pole owner across PA/AL/FL footprint by Friday, flag which have accepted BEAD subgrants, and queue formal rate/timeline challenges where quotes exceed FCC schedule.
