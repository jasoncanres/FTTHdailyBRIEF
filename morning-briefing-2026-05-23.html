Policy / Regulatory

BEAD "Benefit of the Bargain" round forces AZ / NV / PA / AL / FL to re-bid; LEO and FWA now on equal footing with fiber

21/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: NTIA's June 2025 Policy Notice — now executing through the Benefit of the Bargain re-bid — strips fiber's preference and requires every state to evaluate LEO, FWA, DSL, and cable on equal terms against a 100/20 Mbps / 100 ms ceiling. All five Gigapower target states are inside the 90-day re-submission cycle.

Why it matters: Subsidized fiber overbuild competition in AL and FL out-of-AT&T-footprint markets just got cheaper for LEO/FWA bidders; wholesale demand from BEAD-funded ISP tenants in those geographies is at risk of collapsing before it ever lights up.

Action: Government Affairs files written comments this week with the Alabama Department of Economic and Community Affairs and Florida Office of Broadband on the wholesale open-access scoring weight in their revised subgrantee evaluation rubric — anchor argument: long-run cost-per-bit and tenant-ISP optionality vs. LEO depreciation curve.

FCC pole attachment rules effective May 8 — 30-day contractor approval window now live, utilities filing pushback

20/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: Updated FCC pole attachment rules — originally adopted July 2025 — took effect May 8, 2026, imposing tighter make-ready timelines and a 30-day utility approval window for attacher-supplied contractors. Major electric utilities are already at the FCC arguing the contractor rule is unworkable; the February ruling barring cost-shifting of pre-existing pole violations onto attachers stays in force.

Why it matters: Pennsylvania PUC asserts Section 224(c) jurisdiction and historically tracks federal changes — Wilkes-Barre / Scranton aerial routes get a meaningful schedule unlock if Gigapower prequalifies its contractors now. AZ / NV pole owners are FCC-jurisdictional, full benefit available immediately.

Action: Engineering submits its make-ready contractor roster to Salt River Project, APS, NV Energy, and PPL by Tuesday with full safety documentation and references; Permitting drafts a template Section 1.1411 complaint to file if any pole owner misses the 30-day approval window on the first batch.

Eighth Circuit vacates FCC "Digital Discrimination" rule — broadband deployment policy environment swings back toward operators

13/22
Policy / Regulatory🟢 Monitor

What happened: A unanimous Eighth Circuit panel vacated the FCC's 2023 digital discrimination rule on May 6, 2026, removing the disparate-impact compliance overhang on ISP deployment decisions.

Why it matters: Removes a reputational/legal vector that tenant ISPs would have had to manage when selling on Gigapower's network in mixed-income census tracts; reduces compliance cost flow-through into tenant ISP agreements.

Action: Legal updates the tenant ISP Master Services Agreement compliance schedule to strike the digital-discrimination reps before the next Q3 contracting cycle; flag to AT&T Wholesale counterpart for parallel adjustment.
