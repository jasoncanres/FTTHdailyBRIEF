Policy / Regulatory

NTIA clears 50 BEAD Final Proposals; 52 states have signed award agreements, including PA, NV, AZ

20/22
Policy / Regulatory🟡 This Week

What happened: NTIA has approved 54 of 56 Final Proposals and 52 states/territories have signed award agreements as of mid-May. Pennsylvania ($1.16B allocation) leans heavily wired, with Comcast and Verizon taking nearly half the state's awards; Nevada has issued $169.7M of its $417M provisional awards. Construction runs 2026–2030.

Why it matters: BEAD subgrantees will overbuild unserved pockets adjacent to Gigapower's PA and NV footprints, and incumbent winners (Comcast, Verizon) harden their positions before Gigapower's wholesale tenants reach those passings.

Action: Direct Government Affairs to pull the PA and NV provisional award maps this week and overlay them on Wilkes-Barre/Scranton and Clark County build plans to flag overlap and open-access wholesale pitch targets among BEAD winners.

NTIA's Roth signals ~$21B BEAD surplus could fund permitting reform

17/22
Policy / Regulatory🟡 This Week

What happened: Administrator Arielle Roth said states are expected to come in roughly $21B under budget and that NTIA wants "outcome-driven" non-deployment spending, explicitly naming permitting reform as a target use. Guidance was promised for early 2026.

Why it matters: State-funded permitting reform directly compresses Gigapower's biggest non-construction cost and timeline risk — ROW and pole-make-ready cycle times in every active market.

Action: Task Government Affairs to file comments with the AZ, NV, and PA broadband offices advocating non-deployment dollars toward municipal ROW permit digitization and pole make-ready acceleration before the early-2026 guidance hardens.

NTIA BEAD rules extend FCC pole-attachment terms to co-op and municipal poles

17/22
Policy / Regulatory🟢 Monitor

What happened: Co-ops and municipal utilities participating in BEAD as subgrantees must comply with FCC pole-attachment rules for the federal interest period — extending Section 224-style terms to pole owners historically exempt from FCC jurisdiction.

Why it matters: Where Gigapower builds aerial on co-op or muni poles in BEAD areas, this opens a regulated-rate path that previously did not exist, lowering make-ready leverage held by those pole owners.

Action: Have Engineering inventory which poles in PA and AL aerial routes are co-op/muni-owned and cross-reference BEAD subgrantee status to identify where FCC-rate attachment now applies.

Supply Chain

Fiber cable lead times stretch to a full year as AI demand outruns supply 22–25% vs 12–19%

20/22
Supply Chain🔴 Act Today

What happened: North American fiber demand is projected at 22–25% growth in 2026 while supply expands only 12–19%. Lead times have stretched to 20 weeks for large-volume buyers and up to a full year for smaller purchasers, driven by AI data-center buildouts consuming up to 36x the fiber of conventional designs.

Why it matters: Gigapower's expansion beyond 1.5M locations competes for the same glass as hyperscalers and AT&T's accelerating 4M-passing program — unallocated buyers will be rationed last and at the worst pricing.

Action: Have Engineering and Procurement lock 2026–2027 fiber and OLT/ONT allocation under firm purchase commitments this week, before AT&T's parent-program ramp and AI demand close the order window.

AT&T lifts fiber build to 4M passings in 2026, 5M after, and seeks equity partner for NetworkCo wholesale sub

18/22
Parent / JV Watch🟡 This Week

What happened: CEO John Stankey set AT&T's annual fiber pace at 4M passings in 2026 and 5M thereafter, targeting ~60M total fiber locations by 2030. The Lumen Mass Markets/Quantum Fiber assets ($5.75B, closed Feb 2) were placed in a wholly owned subsidiary, NetworkCo, in which AT&T plans to sell a controlling stake to a co-investing equity partner.

Action: Brief the JV/Parent liaison to obtain clarity from AT&T on NetworkCo's market scope versus Gigapower's footprint and confirm no out-of-footprint overlap in PA, AZ, NV, AL, or FL before NetworkCo's equity-partner process closes.

FBA paper maps MSO migration paths from HFC to FTTH, including greenfield overbuild and fiber-deepening

14/22
Market Trends🟢 Monitor

What happened: The Fiber Broadband Association published a technical paper (May 18) outlining how MSOs can transition from coax to FTTH via greenfield overbuilds, phased fiber-deepening, and full system overbuilds — signaling cable operators are moving from defending HFC to planning fiber.

Why it matters: As cable MSOs adopt FTTH, the wholesale open-access model becomes a credible "build-once" alternative to each MSO funding its own overbuild — a direct Gigapower sales narrative.

Action: Have Wholesale BD repackage the FBA migration framework into a one-page open-access pitch positioning Gigapower as the capex-light FTTH path for any cable operator evaluating overbuild in an active market.

16/22
Technology🟡 This Week

Section 5 — My Recommendation

Lock 2026–2027 fiber and OLT/ONT allocation under firm purchase commitments this week. The binding constraint on Gigapower's expansion beyond 1.5M locations is no longer permitting or demand — it is glass. North American fiber demand is running 22–25% against 12–19% supply growth, lead times have stretched to a full year for non-prioritized buyers, and Corning is pre-committing capacity to Meta ($6B) and Nvidia-funded plants that will not relieve the market until 2027. Meanwhile AT&T's parent program is accelerating to 4M passings in 2026 and 5M after, and its new NetworkCo wholesale vehicle will pull from the same supply. Every week Gigapower delays a firm allocation commitment, it moves further back in the queue behind hyperscalers and its own JV parent. Direct Engineering and Procurement to convert forecasted 2026–2027 volume into signed allocation agreements now — price and timeline certainty on material is worth more than the optionality of waiting, because the markets where Brightspeed and BEAD winners are hardening share (NE PA especially) will not wait for cable that arrives in 52 weeks.
