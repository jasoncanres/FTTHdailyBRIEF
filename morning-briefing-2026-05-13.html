NTIA tells ISPs to flag any state BEAD contract that "alters subgrantee rights"↗

20/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: Commerce/NTIA issued a May 2026 memo directing ISPs to notify a federal program officer whenever a state BEAD contract attempts to alter subgrantee rights or omit required federal language. The memo also reinforces the rule that states cannot enforce net neutrality or rate regulation on BEAD recipients, and that permitting must run on a 90-day shot clock with a single point of contact.

Why it matters: This shifts leverage to the operator at the contract table — state broadband offices that try to layer in rate caps, build-out riders, or unfunded mandates can be reported and force re-papering before construction starts.

Action: Have legal redline every active and pending state subgrantee agreement against the NTIA memo this week and file federal program officer notice on any clause that conflicts. Do not sign anything until the redlines clear.
