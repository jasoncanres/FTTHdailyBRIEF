Policy / Regulatory

BEAD subgrants now flowing — Florida and Nevada among states signing ISP agreements

19/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: NTIA has approved Final Proposals for Florida, Nevada, and a cohort of other states, which are now actively signing subgrant agreements with ISPs, co-ops, and municipalities. Most BEAD-funded construction runs 2026–2030. Two of the approved states — FL and NV — are live Gigapower footprint.

Why it matters: Subgrant-funded overbuild reshapes which census blocks become economically contested, and an open-access wholesale platform can host the winning subgrantee rather than compete with it.

Action: Task Wholesale BD this week to pull the FL and NV subgrant award maps against Gigapower's NV/Clark and FL build areas and identify any subgrantee that could ride the network instead of trenching parallel.

FCC opens High-Cost / USF modernization NPRM — comments due August 4

15/22
Policy / Regulatory🟡 This Week

What happened: The Commission released an NPRM to update legacy high-cost mechanisms (CAF BLS, HCLS) so USF dollars target unserved high-speed broadband. Comments due Aug 4, 2026; replies Sept 3, 2026.

Why it matters: Reworked high-cost support changes the subsidy math in the rural fringes adjacent to Gigapower's out-of-footprint greenfield, affecting which edges become buildable.

Action: Have Government Affairs flag whether wholesale-only carriers gain any standing under the revised mechanisms and calendar a comment decision before July 28.

FCC streamlines Broadband Data Collection — National Broadband Map accuracy in play

15/22
Policy / Regulatory🟢 Monitor

What happened: The FCC adopted an R&O and FNPRM to streamline BDC audits, verifications, and challenge processes, aiming to reduce provider burden while keeping the National Broadband Map accurate.

Why it matters: The map governs BEAD eligibility and overbuild challenges; cleaner challenge rules change how fast a market can be contested or defended.

Action: Direct Engineering to confirm Gigapower's availability data filings align with the streamlined verification format before the next BDC window.

Supply Chain

Optical fiber supply gap projected above 6% for 2026 as AI data centers absorb cable

18/22
Supply Chain🔴 Act Today

What happened: Multiple institutions estimate a global optical fiber supply-demand gap exceeding 6% in 2026; China's export unit price spiked toward $80/kg in March. Meta committed up to $6B to Corning and data centers are forecast to need 92,000 route-miles of new fiber — diverting capacity away from FTTH builders.

Why it matters: A wholesale platform building to 2027 cannot afford cable or OLT allocation slippage; hyperscaler demand, not competitor slowdown, sets the price and the queue.

Action: Direct Engineering and procurement to lock 2026–2027 cable and OLT/ONT allocation under firm POs now and confirm Build America/Buy America-compliant supply from Prysmian/Corning before Q3 pricing resets.

Competitive Intelligence

AlixPartners: 66% of fiber operators slowing or stopping builds; 73% signal M&A intent

17/22
Competitive Intelligence🟡 This Week

What happened: A survey of 100 fiber operators and investors found 66% are slowing or halting new builds to protect unit economics — half cut expansion investment, 16% stopped entirely — while 73% say they are likely to engage in M&A within a year.

Why it matters: Capital-starved overbuilders are exactly the ISPs that should ride open-access fiber instead of trenching their own; the retreat is a tenant-acquisition opening for a wholesale carrier with capacity in market.

Action: Have Wholesale BD build a target list of the slowing/stopping overbuilders with announced plans in AZ/NV/SC and open tenant conversations before they default to a competitor's open-access deal.

14/22
Competitive Intelligence🟢 Monitor

What happened: T-Mobile formally launched fiber home internet with multi-year price guarantees, built on its Lumos JV and ~755K customers acquired via Metronet, pairing fiber with its FWA base.

Why it matters: Multi-year price locks reset retail expectations in any market a Gigapower tenant ISP sells into, pressuring tenant ARPU assumptions.

Action: Brief tenant ISPs (AT&T Fiber, Flume, Dojo) on T-Mobile's price-lock terms so Section 4 D2C positioning in overlapping markets accounts for it.

Market Trends

Fiber take rates hit 46.5% on primary passings — but ~61% where providers compete

16/22
Market Trends🟡 This Week

What happened: US fiber is now marketed to 84.6M unique homes (98.4M total passings). Average take rate on primary passings rose to 46.5%, and total take-up climbs to roughly 61% in markets with competing fiber providers.

Why it matters: The competitive-market uplift is the empirical case for open access — multiple ISPs on one network lift aggregate take above single-operator economics, which is precisely the Gigapower thesis.

Action: Have Wholesale BD turn the 46.5% vs 61% spread into the lead slide of the tenant-ISP recruitment deck and quantify the multi-tenant take premium for AZ/NV markets.

12/22
Market Trends🟢 Monitor

Why it matters: AT&T's in-footprint acceleration sets the anchor-tenant demand curve that underwrites Gigapower's out-of-footprint build pace.

Action: Confirm with JV/Parent liaison that the 1M/yr in-footprint pace does not pull engineering or cable allocation away from Gigapower greenfield in 2026.

15/22
Technology & Growth🟢 Monitor

Section 5 — My Recommendation

Lock 2026–2027 fiber-cable and OLT allocation under firm purchase orders this week — treat it as the single gating constraint on the 2027 build. The supply signal is unambiguous: a global optical-fiber gap above 6%, China export prices spiking toward $80/kg, Meta's $6B Corning commitment, and 92,000 route-miles of data-center demand all point to hyperscalers — not competitors — setting the queue. The AlixPartners finding that 66% of operators are slowing builds is a trap if read as supply relief; that capacity is being absorbed upstream by AI, not freed for FTTH. Gigapower is building to a 2027 completion across nine states with penetration already ahead of plan, so a cable or OLT slip is the one thing that converts a winning plan into a missed window. Direct Engineering and procurement to convert 2026–2027 demand into firm, BABA-compliant POs with Prysmian and Corning before Q3 pricing resets, and have the JV/Parent liaison confirm AT&T's 1M-location-per-year in-footprint acceleration is not competing for the same allocation. Every other opportunity this week — tenant recruitment off the competitor retreat, the SC/NC footprint expansion, the MDU push with Dojo — depends on having glass in the ground on schedule.
