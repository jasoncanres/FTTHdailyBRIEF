Policy / Regulatory

FCC pole attachment reform rules took legal effect May 7 — make-ready timelines tighten, full-cost-of-replacement banned

22/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: The FCC's Fifth Report and Order pole attachment reforms became enforceable May 7, 2026 (OMB clearance April 17). The order shortens large-request timelines, accelerates contractor approval, and codifies February's Comcast v. Appalachian Power ruling that pole owners cannot bill attachers for the full cost of replacement poles — only incremental cost above what the owner would have paid. NTIA's January 2026 BEAD terms extended these rate caps and timelines to cooperative and municipal pole owners receiving BEAD dollars.

Why it matters: NE PA cluster (Wilkes-Barre/Scranton) is PPL Electric aerial-heavy and AL out-of-AT&T-footprint targets ride co-op and municipal poles. Old make-ready quotes and timelines are immediately renegotiable; bonded cost models that assumed full replacement liability are now overstated.

Action: Engineering and Legal — pull every active pole attachment quote in PA, AL, and FL today, identify make-ready estimates that include full-replacement loading, and issue refile letters to PPL Electric, Alabama Power, and any BEAD-receiving co-op pole owners by close of business Friday May 15.

Alabama BEAD final proposal cleared — $460M to 63 projects, 71% fiber; AT&T captures only $93.2M

20/22
Policy / Regulatory🟡 This Week

What happened: Governor Ivey confirmed NTIA approval of Alabama's BEAD Final Proposal in March; Fox10 reported May 1 that ADECA is moving $1.4B in combined federal funding into subgrantee execution. 71% of the 63 awarded projects are fiber, 24% LEO, 5% HFC. Comcast leads with $157M; BellSouth Telecommunications (AT&T) received $93.2M for fiber — a notably modest parent share in a state where Gigapower has Intelligence-stage targets.

Why it matters: AT&T's $93.2M footprint defines the BEAD-protected zones in Alabama. Gigapower's out-of-AT&T-footprint thesis is now mappable against actual subgrant geography. The 24% LEO/5% HFC slice means a meaningful BEAD-funded coverage gap remains for wholesale-friendly fiber to fill.

Action: Wholesale BD and Government Affairs — request the executed ADECA subgrant map by Tuesday May 19, overlay against the AL Intelligence list, and identify three municipalities where neither AT&T nor a BEAD subgrantee will reach. Brief JV liaison by EOW May 22.

22/22
Supply Chain🔴 Act Today

U.S. FTTH market nears 100M passings; fiber Capex up 5-15% on bonus depreciation restoration

13/22
Market Trends🟢 Monitor

What happened: RVA and Broadband Communities report 11.8M new homes passed in 2025 brought the U.S. total to 98.3M FTTH passings — 100M crossed in Q2 2026. Restoration of 100% bonus depreciation in 2026 drives a projected 5-15% Capex bump across the industry. Uniti's Kinetic reported 29.1% fiber penetration, +120 bps YoY; fiber ARPU +5%.

Why it matters: The market is approaching saturation in the easy passings. Wholesale open-access economics improve as overbuild competition intensifies — multi-tenant fiber spreads tail-end Capex across two or three ISP revenue streams instead of one. Take rates of 29-35% are now industry-standard for mature fiber footprints; the AZ markets should be modeling against that benchmark, not the 20% legacy assumption.

Action: Wholesale BD and Finance — re-run the AZ Phase 1 take-rate model against a 29% Year-3 penetration assumption per tenant and a multi-tenant blended take-rate ceiling of 45%. Brief at JV finance committee June 5.
