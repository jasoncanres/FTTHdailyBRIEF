Section 1 — Intelligence Feed

Policy / Regulatory

SUCCESS for BEAD Act names wholesale fiber an eligible use for ~$20B in non-deployment funds

21/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: With NTIA's June 2025 restructuring now fully in force and 52 states/territories holding signed award agreements as of mid-May 2026, attention has shifted to non-deployment dollars — potentially $20B-plus in surplus. The SUCCESS for BEAD Act explicitly lists wholesale fiber, mobile wireless infrastructure, subsea cables, and 911 upgrades as eligible non-deployment uses, with NTIA guidance pending.

Why it matters: A federal statute naming "wholesale fiber" as an eligible BEAD use is a direct funding on-ramp for Gigapower's open-access model in every active state.

Action: Government Affairs — open positioning files this week with the AZ, NV, PA, AL, and FL state broadband offices framing Gigapower as a wholesale-fiber recipient for non-deployment surplus; brief Wholesale BD before NTIA's guidance drops.

Federal watchdog finds 2025 BEAD restructuring broke the law — re-litigation risk on tech-neutral rules

12/22
Policy / Regulatory🟢 Monitor

What happened: A federal watchdog concluded NTIA's removal of the fiber-first preference and labor/climate requirements exceeded statutory authority. The finding does not reverse the rules but injects litigation and reversal risk into the tech-neutral framework now governing state awards.

Why it matters: If the fiber preference is judicially restored, the competitive calculus for fiber-vs-LEO/FWA in Gigapower's greenfield states shifts back toward fiber.

Action: JV/Parent liaison — flag to AT&T regulatory affairs for a 30-day watch; no spend decision until the legal track clarifies.

Supply Chain

Corning quoting ~52-week lead times on BABA cable, 60+ weeks on ribbon as AI data centers absorb supply

20/22
Supply Chain🔴 Act Today

What happened: Corning is quoting roughly 52 weeks for domestic BABA-compliant G.652D loose-tube and 60-plus weeks for ribbon. Global data-center fiber demand jumped 75.9% year-on-year in 2025 and is projected to hit 30% of all global fiber demand by 2027, with only three U.S. BABA-compliant glass makers (Corning, OFS, Prysmian).

Why it matters: Construction schedules in Mesa/Chandler/Gilbert and the NE PA cluster are exposed to a 12-month-plus cable lead time that AI hyperscaler demand is actively worsening.

Action: Engineering and Construction — place or extend cable allocations against the AZ and PA build calendars this week; do not assume sub-12-month availability in any 2027 schedule.
