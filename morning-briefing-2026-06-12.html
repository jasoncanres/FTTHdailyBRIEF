NTIA weighs ~$21B in BEAD savings toward permitting reform; non-deployment guidance still pending

16/22
Policy / Regulatory🟡 This Week

What happened: NTIA reports ~$21B in projected savings from the "Benefit of the Bargain" BEAD restructuring and is actively weighing how to redeploy it. Administrator Roth has floated permitting reform as a priority use, alongside supplemental unserved-location rounds and middle-mile backhaul. Previously approved non-deployment uses remain unreimbursable pending new guidance.

Why it matters: Permitting-reform dollars directed at municipal ROW and pole-attachment bottlenecks would directly compress Gigapower's market-entry timelines in AZ, NV, PA, AL, and FL.

Action: Task Government Affairs to file comment with NTIA this week advocating BEAD savings fund municipal permitting/pole-make-ready acceleration, citing Gigapower's NE-PA and Maricopa permitting queues as evidence.

Amazon signs multibillion-dollar Corning fiber deal — third AI mega-contract crowding FTTH supply

20/22
Supply Chain🔴 Act Today

What happened: Amazon announced June 8 a multiyear, multibillion-dollar agreement for Corning optical fiber and connectivity to power AWS AI data centers, adding 1,000 NC manufacturing jobs. It is Corning's third AI mega-deal of 2026 — after Meta ($6B, January) and Nvidia ($3.2B, May). Corning Optical Communications sales rose 36% YoY in Q1 2026, almost entirely on AI demand.

Market Trends

FTTH now passes 57% of U.S. addresses, up from 37% in 2024 — overbuild risk rising

13/22
Market Trends🟢 Monitor

What happened: Fiber now reaches 57% of U.S. addresses, a 20-point gain in two years. AT&T led the trailing 12 months with ~6.5M new passings, Frontier added 3.2M, and regional providers a combined 4.1M.

Why it matters: As single-provider fiber coverage saturates, the remaining greenfield shrinks and multi-ISP overbuild economics — exactly Gigapower's wholesale thesis — become the differentiated play versus chasing virgin territory.

Action: Strategy: pressure-test the wholesale open-access pitch deck against a "57% passed" market — lead tenant-ISP solicitations with multi-ISP demand aggregation, not first-to-market framing.

Section 2 — M&A Watch

T-Mobile adds two more fiber JVs (~$2.7B): Oak Hill/GoNetspeed + Greenlight, plus i3 — anchor-tenant model spreads

17/22
M&A🟢 Monitor

What happened: T-Mobile committed ~$2.7B to two more fiber joint ventures — a 50/50 JV with Oak Hill Capital to acquire GoNetspeed and Greenlight Networks, plus an i3 partnership — expected to close in H1 2027. This follows the closed KKR/Metronet JV, where Metronet became a wholesale provider and T-Mobile took 100% of residential retail as anchor tenant.

Why it matters: The carrier-anchored-tenant-over-wholesale-fiber structure is now the dominant U.S. scaling pattern — the same architecture as Gigapower. Every new entrant validates the model but also bids up fiber asset multiples and competes for the same infra capital (KKR, Oak Hill, BlackRock).

Action: JV/Parent liaison + Wholesale BD: brief BlackRock on the T-Mobile/Oak Hill comps to benchmark Gigapower's capital cost and tenant-economics terms against the latest 2026 wholesale-fiber deal structures.

Section 3 — Technology & Growth Strategy

Fiber becomes AI's bottleneck: 36x strand density per AI rack pushes multicore fiber into the access conversation

16/22
Technology🟡 This Week

What happened: AI racks require ~36x the fiber of legacy CPU racks; 72-GPU Blackwell nodes need 16x the fiber of standard cloud switches. Corning's multicore fiber packs multiple cores in a single 125-micron strand for 4x capacity per fiber — initially a data-center play now being eyed for high-density access and metro routes.

Why it matters: AI demand is dragging the entire fiber R&D and capacity roadmap toward hyperscaler needs. Higher-count and multicore cable that lowers cost-per-home-passed in dense MDU and metro builds is the strategic upside for wholesale operators if specified early.

Action: Engineering: add high-count / multicore cable options to the next OSP design standard review and request Corning roadmap briefings for metro-MDU applicability in Las Vegas and Maricopa builds.
