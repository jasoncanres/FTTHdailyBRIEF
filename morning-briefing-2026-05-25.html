Policy / Regulatory

FCC pole attachment rules took effect May 8 — 30-day contractor default-approval, cost-causation locked in

22/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: The FCC's accelerating wireline broadband deployment order published in the Federal Register May 7 and took effect May 8. Utilities must respond within 30 days to attacher requests to use new contractors for make-ready prep; silence is deemed approval. New 120-day window applies for 3,000–6,000-pole batches. Pole owners cannot force attachers to pay to remediate pre-existing safety or structural violations.

Why it matters: Aerial spans across Mesa/Chandler/Gilbert and the Wilkes-Barre/Scranton cluster now have a federal-law clock running on every pole owner Gigapower attaches to — and the cost-causation principle removes the largest swing-cost line item from make-ready estimates.

Action: Engineering and Legal jointly issue an FCC-Section-1.1411-compliant batch letter to APS, SRP, Salt River Project, PPL Electric Utilities, and PA-NEPA pole-owning ILECs this week tendering Gigapower's pre-approved contractor list and demanding written acknowledgement of the 30-day default-approval clock.
