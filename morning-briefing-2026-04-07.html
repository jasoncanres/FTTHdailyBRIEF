BEAD Tech Neutrality Expansion — LEO Satellites and Fixed Wireless Now Eligible
🟡 This Week 18/22
Policy / Regulatory
What Happened

The NTIA's 2025 BEAD restructuring permanently expanded grant eligibility to include Low Earth Orbit (LEO) satellites and Fixed Wireless Access (FWA) alongside fiber. This neutrality shift means non-fiber operators can now compete for the same BEAD rural subsidies previously dominated by FTTH builds. The NTIA also rescinded all previously approved "non-deployment" funding (digital equity, device programs, workforce development), creating policy uncertainty for state offices on how to reallocate those dollars.

Why It Matters

FWA and LEO providers can now bid against fiber operators in BEAD auctions, compressing your cost-of-capital advantage in rural markets and forcing a sharper value proposition in proposal scoring.

Recommended Action
Audit your BEAD proposal scoring model this week to ensure fiber's speed, latency, and longevity advantages are explicitly quantified versus LEO/FWA alternatives. Coordinate with your state broadband office to understand their scoring weights — if fiber isn't clearly differentiated in the proposal, you risk losing on cost alone.
Supply Chain
Fiber Optic Cable Prices Up 80–650% Since Late 2025 — Supply Crunch Accelerating
🔴 Act Today 20/22
Supply Chain
What Happened

Market data through March 9, 2026 shows G.657A1 fiber trading at $22/km (up from $12–14/km in mid-2025 — an 80% increase) and G.657A2 at $35/km (up from $18–22/km, a 90% increase). Some G.652D fiber grades surged 400–650% in RMB-denominated spot markets. The primary drivers are twofold: explosive military drone demand for fiber-guided munitions consuming global preform output, and AI data center construction creating simultaneous civilian demand. Prices are expected to remain elevated or rise further through Q2 2026, with possible easing in Q3–Q4 if military procurement slows or new drawing capacity comes online. Note: major operators with multi-year Corning agreements (AT&T's $1B Corning deal from 2024) are insulated; operators without long-term contracts are fully exposed to spot prices.

Why It Matters

Greenfield FTTH operators without locked supply agreements face a 30–50%+ increase in fiber cable line items on active and planned builds — a direct hit to project IRR and BEAD budget compliance.

Recommended Action
Call Corning, CommScope, and Prysmian today to assess multi-year contract availability at current pricing. If Q2 prices are quoted as open, lock volume at today's rate regardless of schedule certainty — a forward commitment now is cheaper than spot procurement in Q3. Revisit BEAD project budgets to ensure fiber cable costs are reflected at current market rates, not 2024 baselines.

Section 3 — Technology & Growth Strategy

XGS-PON Is at Peak Global Deployment — 25G/50G-PON Roadmap Is Now Active
🔴 Act Today 20/22
Technology & Growth Strategy Available Now
What's Emerging

2026 represents the global peak deployment year for XGS-PON (10G symmetrical PON), which has become the default standard for new FTTH builds worldwide. Industry consensus positions XGS-PON as the mass-market upgrade path through at least 2030. Simultaneously, 25G-PON deployments are beginning to accelerate, with operators like HKBN in Hong Kong upgrading directly from GPON to 25G without additional network elements. The roadmap beyond 25G includes 50G-PON, designed for AI-heavy applications, holographic conferencing, and industrial IoT. CableLabs has launched an interoperability working group for XGS-PON and 25G-PON, targeting vendor-neutral ONU/OLT compatibility.

Why It Matters for Growth

Any greenfield FTTH build launched in 2026 without XGS-PON-ready OLT equipment is already behind the curve — and any build using GPON-only hardware is deploying stranded infrastructure that will require costly upgrades within 3–5 years as customer speed expectations escalate alongside AI service demands.

How to Apply It
Deploy XGS-PON now — it's the only defensible greenfield build standard in 2026. Specify XGS-PON OLTs with 25G upgrade path capability in all current RFQs. If you're already in a GPON design cycle, halt it and re-spec. The marginal cost delta between GPON and XGS-PON hardware is smaller than the cost of re-engineering the network in three years.
