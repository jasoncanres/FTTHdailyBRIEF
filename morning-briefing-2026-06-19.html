Section 1 — Intelligence Feed

Policy / Regulatory

NTIA steers BEAD non-deployment funds toward permitting reform, locks technology-neutral build rules

16/22
Policy / Regulatory🟡 This Week

What happened: NTIA Administrator Roth signaled that states' remaining and non-deployment BEAD dollars may be redirected to permitting reform, while the technology-neutral, lowest-cost-option framework (which stripped the original fiber preference in mid-2025) is now baked into approved state final proposals — 54 of 56 states/territories approved, 52 with funds released.

Why it matters: Permitting-reform money lowers the single largest non-construction cost driver for greenfield FTTH, and a technology-neutral regime invites FWA/LEO to contest passings Gigapower would otherwise wholesale uncontested.

Action: Direct Government Affairs to file comments with the AZ, NV, and PA broadband offices by July 1 advocating that permitting-reform funds flow to ROW/pole-attachment process streamlining in Maricopa, Clark, Luzerne, and Lackawanna counties.

Supply Chain

Corning lifts US fiber capacity 50%+ — but NVIDIA, Amazon, and Meta deals claim priority allocation

18/22
Supply Chain🔴 Act Today

What happened: Corning is expanding US fiber production 50%+ and optical connectivity 10x across new NC/TX plants, anchored by a multiyear NVIDIA partnership, a $6B Meta agreement, and a multibillion-dollar Amazon deal struck June 8. Optical Communications sales rose 36% YoY in Q1, almost entirely AI-driven; an AI rack consumes up to 16x the fiber of a legacy cloud switch rack.

Why it matters: Hyperscaler AI buildouts now sit ahead of FTTH access builders in the fiber, conduit, and trenching-labor queue — the same Maricopa-County labor pool Lightpath and the data-center cluster are already draining.

Action: Have Engineering and Procurement lock 2027 single-mode fiber and microduct allocations with Corning/Prysmian under firm contract before July 31, and price in a 1–2 quarter lead-time cushion on AZ/NV build plans now.

Competitive Intelligence🟡 This Week

Action: Task Permitting with pulling Mesa and Chandler ROW permit-queue timelines this week to quantify Lightpath-driven slippage, and have Construction confirm boring-crew availability through Q4 before committing new AZ build starts.

Market Trends🟢 Monitor

Why it matters: In a saturating market, multi-ISP open access is the differentiator — more tenant ISPs per passing lifts blended take rate without Gigapower carrying retail churn risk.
