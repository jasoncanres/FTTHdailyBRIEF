Section 1 — Intelligence Feed

Policy / Regulatory

FCC pole-attachment rules took effect this month — replacement-cost burden shifts off attachers

22/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: FCC's updated pole-attachment rules took effect in May 2026, establishing fixed timelines for utility responses and streamlined contractor approvals. In February the Commission ruled in the Comcast/Appalachian Power case that pole owners cannot force attachers to fund replacement of poles with pre-existing safety violations — attachers pay only the incremental cost of a stronger pole.

Why it matters: Reduces Gigapower aerial CapEx exposure in PA (Wilkes-Barre/Scranton) and AL where joint-use pole conditions are unknown. Hardens leverage against pole owners that previously demanded full make-ready payment.

Action: Engineering + Legal: re-open all open pole-attachment make-ready estimates in PA and AL by Friday, May 29 and issue refund/credit demands citing the new FCC framework for any pre-existing-violation charges already paid.

Alabama awards $460M BEAD — 71% fiber, 63 projects, construction starts year-end 2026

21/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: Governor Ivey and ADECA announced final BEAD subgrantee awards: $460M across 63 projects to reach ~92,000 unserved AL locations, 71% fiber / 24% LEO / 5% HFC. Alabama is preserving $800M+ of its $1.4B allocation for future non-deployment uses. Subgrantees have a 4-year build clock.

Why it matters: AL is a confirmed Gigapower expansion target. BEAD subgrantees will saturate the unserved CAM list before Gigapower lands; the live question is whether Gigapower competes for served-but-underserved overbuild adjacencies and whether subgrantees become tenant ISP candidates.

Action: Wholesale BD: pull the ADECA subgrantee list this week and identify the 5–8 fiber awardees whose footprints abut Gigapower's AL target metros. Initiate wholesale-tenant or backhaul conversations by June 15.

Pennsylvania BEAD $711M executed — 64% fiber, Brightspeed nearly complete in five PA communities

21/22
Policy / Regulatory🟡 This Week

What happened: PBDA-NTIA agreement executed in February for $711.3M of $793M provisional awards across 129,941 PA locations (64% fiber / 18% LEO / 14% FWA / 4% HFC). FBA confirmed May 6 that Brightspeed's PA fiber buildout is nearly complete with five fully fiber-ready PA communities. Comcast pulled $229M for HFC; Verizon $121M for fiber.

Why it matters: Direct overbuild risk in Wilkes-Barre/Scranton. Brightspeed and Verizon are racing into the Lackawanna/Luzerne CAMs ahead of Gigapower's NE-PA construction start. Take-rate ceiling shrinks every month Gigapower delays.

Action: Government Affairs + Permitting: cross-reference the Luzerne/Lackawanna BEAD award maps against Gigapower's NE-PA build plan this week and escalate any overlap to JV leadership for go/no-go by May 28.

Supply Chain

Fiber cable lead times 20 weeks for large buyers, up to one year for small — AI demand 36× CPU-rack baseline

21/22
Supply Chain🔴 Act Today

What happened: Corning is selling fiber as fast as it makes it. Lead times: 6–10 weeks routine, 20 weeks for large-volume orders, up to 52 weeks for smaller buyers. Meta–Corning $6B multi-year supply deal (through 2030) further tightens western capacity. AI racks consume 36× the fiber of a CPU rack; Blackwell-class nodes consume 16× a standard switch rack.

Why it matters: Gigapower's 2027 construction in AZ/AL/FL will run head-on into hyperscaler procurement. Every quarter Gigapower waits to lock allocations, the marginal cost-per-foot climbs and the build window narrows.

Action: JV/Parent liaison: route a 2027–2028 fiber-cable framework-agreement request through AT&T Global Supply Chain by June 6, sized for Mesa/Chandler/Gilbert + NE-PA + AL/FL Phase-1 footprint at minimum.

20/22
