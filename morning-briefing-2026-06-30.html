Section 1 — Intelligence Feed

Policy / Regulatory

NTIA holds technology-neutral BEAD line; non-deployment dollars stay frozen pending Lutnick guidance

16/22
Policy / Regulatory🟡 This Week

What happened: The technology-neutral BEAD framework remains in force, stripping the prior fiber preference and letting fixed wireless and LEO compete on the 100/20 Mbps benchmark. Non-deployment funds (workforce, digital literacy) remain rescinded, and Commerce is weighing whether leftover dollars go to permitting reform or back to Treasury. A federal watchdog found the 2025 rule changes broke the law, but the rules stand.

Why it matters: Tech-neutrality erodes the subsidized-fiber moat in rural edges around Gigapower's AL/FL footprint, where wireless ISPs can now underbid on the same passings.

Action: Government Affairs — map BEAD-eligible census blocks adjacent to AL/FL build zones this week and flag any where a wireless awardee could strand a planned Gigapower passing before permitting locks.

52 states sign BEAD award agreements; first shovels expected summer 2026

15/22
Policy / Regulatory🟢 Monitor

What happened: NTIA's dashboard shows 54 final proposals approved, 52 states with signed award agreements, and construction starting as early as this summer. That releases a wave of subsidized passings nationwide, competing for the same fiber, crews, and boring capacity Gigapower needs.

Why it matters: BEAD build starts will tighten an already-strained labor and materials market exactly as Gigapower scales AZ/NV and breaks ground in NEPA.

Action: Construction — pull BEAD award maps for AZ, NV, and PA by Friday and pre-book trenching/boring subcontractors before BEAD awardees lock regional crew capacity in Q3.

Supply Chain

Fiber lead times blow out to 52+ weeks as Corning reallocates glass to AI data centers

21/22
Supply Chain🔴 Act Today

What happened: Loose-tube fiber from Corning is now quoted at 52-week lead times and ribbon fiber at 60-plus weeks. Manufacturers have shifted lines from standard G.652D telecom fiber to higher-margin G.657A for AI data centers, and Corning has effectively stopped selling raw glass preform to third parties. Multiple small and mid-size BEAD ISPs have had cable orders cancelled outright, unable to source BABA-compliant glass.

Why it matters: A 12-month fiber lead time directly resets Gigapower's build schedule and cost basis in every active market — this is the single largest threat to the 2026–27 passings plan.

Action: Engineering + Procurement — convene a supply war-room this week, confirm Gigapower's allocation tier and PO coverage with Corning/Prysmian/OFS for all 2027 passings, and lock a secondary supplier before quotes stretch past 60 weeks.

Corning locks hyperscaler glass; Nvidia pumps $300M into new NC/TX fiber plants

17/22
Supply Chain🟡 This Week

What happened: Corning signed a $6B Meta supply agreement and disclosed two more hyperscaler deals of similar scale in Q1 earnings; Nvidia invested $300M to fund three new Corning fiber plants in North Carolina and Texas. New domestic capacity is years out, and near-term output is committed to data centers.

Why it matters: Hyperscaler take-or-pay contracts sit ahead of telecom in the queue — relief for FTTH builders won't arrive until the new lines ramp in 2027–28.

Action: Procurement — negotiate a multi-year take-or-pay allocation now rather than spot buying; price in a 15–20% materials escalation on all 2027 build pro formas before the next JV capital review.

Section 3 — Technology & Growth Strategy

AI data centers consume 36x the fiber; US route miles must nearly double to 187K by 2029

18/22
Tech & Growth🟡 This Week

What happened: FBA research shows AI racks need ~36x more fiber than standard servers; data-center fiber demand grew ~76% YoY and is projected to reach 30% of global demand by 2027. US fiber route miles need to climb from ~95,000 to ~187,000 by 2029. Data-center siting is gated on fiber proximity and access.

Why it matters: This is the demand wave behind the supply crunch — and an open-access wholesale growth lane. Gigapower's NV and AZ footprint sits near active data-center corridors that need middle-mile and interconnect fiber.

Action: Wholesale BD — commission a 30-day study on monetizing Gigapower's Las Vegas/Maricopa routes for data-center interconnect and hyperscaler backhaul, and present a wholesale data-center product line to the JV at the next strategy review.
