Policy / Regulatory

FCC opens inquiry on whether certified states are effectively regulating pole attachments (DA-26-579, June 11)

20/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: The FCC released DA-26-579 on June 11 seeking comment on whether states that have certified to regulate pole attachments are doing so "transparently and effectively," and on actions the Commission could take where state regimes are inadequate. It follows the FCC's Accelerated Docket resolving a Comcast pole cost-allocation dispute in under 60 days via the Rapid Broadband Assessment Team to protect BEAD builds.

Why it matters: Pole make-ready cost and timeline is the single biggest controllable variable in Gigapower's aerial-build economics across all five active states (AZ, NV, PA, AL, FL) — three of which are state-certified jurisdictions where this proceeding could reset the rate and dispute framework.

Action: Direct Government Affairs to file comments by the docket deadline documenting pole make-ready delays in AZ, NV, PA, AL, and FL, and to map which of the five are self-certified vs. FCC-jurisdictional before any aerial permit package goes out this quarter.

BEAD clears the gate: 54 states approved, 52 funded, construction begins summer 2026

16/22
Policy / Regulatory🟡 This Week

What happened: NTIA reports 54 states and territories have approved final proposals, 52 have signed award agreements with funds released, and first BEAD-funded construction is expected to start as early as this summer under the January 2026 revised terms.

Why it matters: BEAD-subsidized overbuilders entering rural and edge census blocks in Gigapower's footprint states compete for the same trenching crews, conduit, and pole make-ready slots Gigapower needs — and can undercut take-rate math in any contested passing.

Action: Task Permitting to pull the approved BEAD subgrantee award maps for NV, AZ, PA, AL, and FL this week and overlay them against Gigapower's planned passings to flag direct-overbuild and crew-contention zones.
