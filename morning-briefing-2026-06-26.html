Pennsylvania BEAD locked: $711M, 20 entities, 74,878 fiber locations across 69 projects

20/22
Policy / Regulatory🟡 This Week

What happened: NTIA executed PA's BEAD agreement to deploy $711,295,843 through 20 approved entities across 69 projects, connecting 129,356 unserved/underserved locations — 74,878 of them by fiber. Subsidized builds reshape the competitive map in Gigapower's NEPA target counties (Luzerne, Lackawanna).

Why it matters: Subsidized fiber in unserved PA census blocks shrinks the addressable greenfield where Gigapower's wholesale economics work and hands incumbents a capital advantage in the same metros.

Action: Have Government Affairs pull the PA Broadband Development Authority award list this week and overlay the 69 project polygons against the Wilkes-Barre/Scranton intelligence map before permitting spend is committed.

BEAD "fiber-first" dropped; 54 states approved; construction starts summer 2026

18/22
Policy / Regulatory🟡 This Week

What happened: 54 of 56 states/territories have NTIA-approved final proposals and 52 have signed award agreements. June restructuring killed the original fiber-first mandate, opening BEAD dollars to LEO and fixed wireless. First BEAD-funded construction begins this summer.

Why it matters: Technology-neutral BEAD means alternative-tech overbuilds can now beat fiber to subsidized passings, compressing the window where Gigapower's open-access fiber is the obvious wholesale substrate.

Action: Direct Wholesale BD to model take-rate sensitivity in AZ/NV/FL BEAD blocks under a fixed-wireless overbuild scenario and flag any market where the fiber thesis no longer clears the hurdle rate.
