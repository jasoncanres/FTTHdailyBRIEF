Policy / Regulatory

NTIA strips BEAD of fiber preference and nonstatutory mandates — program now technology-neutral

20/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: A June 2026 NTIA public notice formalized the rework of BEAD's $42.45B program, ending prioritization of fiber in favor of a technology-neutral selection regime and eliminating nonstatutory requirements covering labor/workforce, net neutrality, local coordination, and climate resilience. 54 of 56 states/territories now hold final-proposal approval; deployment runs 2026–2030.

Why it matters: Open-access fiber loses its automatic scoring edge against FWA and LEO, but Gigapower's already-built passings let tenant ISPs win on delivered cost-per-location rather than on a fiber set-aside.

Action: Direct Government Affairs to re-map BEAD subgrant scoring in NV, AZ, PA, AL, FL by Friday and flag every CAI/location where Gigapower's existing route beats a greenfield FWA bid on total cost.

BEAD subgrantees must waive enforcement of state net-neutrality and rate-regulation laws statewide

19/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: Under the updated June 2026 rules, states must agree not to enforce net-neutrality or broadband rate-regulation laws against BEAD participants — and the condition applies across the ISP's entire in-state footprint, not just funded routes.

Why it matters: Any tenant ISP that takes BEAD on Gigapower fiber drags statewide rate/neutrality obligations into the wholesale relationship, which must be papered in the master lease.

Action: Task JV/Parent legal liaison to add a BEAD-participation compliance rep and indemnity to the tenant-ISP master lease template before any subgrant-backed tenant signs.

Nevada unlocks $170M of $416M BEAD allocation; construction begins mid-2026 in Clark County

22/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: Nevada's NTIA-approved final proposal releases $170M of its $416M allocation to reach 28,000+ homes, businesses, and anchor institutions, with deployment beginning mid-2026 — directly overlapping Gigapower's live Las Vegas / Clark County market.

Why it matters: Subgrantees facing 2026 build clocks in Clark County can lease Gigapower's existing open-access fiber instead of overbuilding, turning a regulatory deadline into wholesale demand in an active market.

Action: Wholesale BD to pull the Nevada subgrantee award list this week and pitch Gigapower's Las Vegas footprint as the BEAD infrastructure layer before subgrantees commit to self-build.
