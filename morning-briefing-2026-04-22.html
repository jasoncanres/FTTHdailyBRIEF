Section 3 — Technology & Growth Strategy

🟡 This WeekAvailable Now

Why it matters for growth: Specifying GPON today locks your capex into a sub-premium tier and limits multi-gig pricing power through the 2030s — you cannot charge for 2G/5G on GPON.

Action — Deploy Now: Specify XGS-PON baseline on every new OLT chassis. If you have legacy GPON shelves under 3 years old, plan the ONT-side migration to XGS this year so you can launch 2G/5G tiers. If 3+ years old, accelerate the chassis swap. The ROI is the multi-gig tier revenue you can't otherwise charge for.

Gigapower joins open-access peers (Utopia, Intrepid, Si-Fi, Tillman) — wholesale FTTH is now a real model, not a thought experiment

19/22
🟡 This WeekEarly Deployment

What's emerging: Gigapower (AT&T/BlackRock JV) went officially open-access, joining Utopia Fiber, Intrepid Fiber, Si-Fi Networks, and Tillman. Open-access means the same underlying fiber infrastructure is leased to multiple retail ISPs.

Why it matters for growth: Open access decouples capex from retail economics. If you can be the wholesale layer, your ARPU floor is set by the highest-bidding retail ISP in each market. If you are only retail, open access drives down your brand premium.

Action — Pilot Test: Run an open-access revenue model on one MDU-dense market in the next 90 days. If unit economics exceed 1.4x your retail-only model, pivot that market's strategy. Why: you capture higher take rates with multiple retail brands and still own the infrastructure asset.
