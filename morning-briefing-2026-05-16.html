Policy / Regulatory

FCC pole attachment overhaul took effect May 8 — 30-day contractor approval clocks, 120-day make-ready windows for 3,000–6,000-pole orders

21/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: FCC's revised Part 1.1411 pole attachment rules became effective May 8, 2026. Utilities now have 30 days to approve a new attacher contractor (default approval if silent), and a new 120-day make-ready window applies to orders between 3,000 and 6,000 poles. Attachers must give 60 days' notice for orders over 300 poles or forfeit the timeline protections. NTIA's January BEAD terms extend these rules to co-op and municipal pole owners taking BEAD funding.

Why it matters: NEPA aerial builds in Luzerne/Lackawanna and any AL/FL co-op pole routes just got a federal speed clock — but only if Gigapower files the right notices on time.

Action: Engineering and Permitting jointly issue a one-page pole-attachment SOP by Wednesday, May 20: 60-day pre-application notice template, contractor pre-approval letter, and a tracking field in the permitting tracker for the 30/120-day clocks. Re-file any pending NEPA pole orders to capture the timeline protections.

NTIA hits 50 BEAD final-proposal approvals; states must restrict net-neutrality and rate enforcement against participants

19/22
Policy / Regulatory🟡 This Week

What happened: Assistant Secretary Arielle Roth announced 50 BEAD Final Proposals approved, NIST has signed off on 52, and 51 states have returned award agreements. NTIA simultaneously issued guidance demanding state contracts bar enforcement of state net-neutrality and rate-regulation statutes against BEAD subgrantees. AZ, NV, PA, AL, FL are all through final approval.

Why it matters: BEAD-subsidized overbuilders will be in Gigapower's footprint by mid-2026 with a federal regulatory shield that Gigapower doesn't carry on its commercial builds. Wholesale tenant economics need to model BEAD-subsidized retail competition into take-rate assumptions.

Action: Wholesale BD pulls each state's BEAD subgrantee award list by Friday, May 22 for AZ/NV/PA/AL/FL. Flag any subgrantee whose service area overlaps an active Gigapower census block. Brief JV finance on take-rate sensitivity.

20/22
Supply Chain🔴 Act Today

What happened: Corning ribbon-cable lead times now sit at 60-plus weeks; standard loose-tube at 20 weeks for tier-1 buyers, up to a year for smaller orders. Market & Take-Rate Trends

FTTH overbuild headed to 75% of cable HHs by 2028; three-fiber markets settle below 30% per operator

17/22
Market Trends🟡 This Week

What happened: Industry analysts (S&P, EY, PwC, Cartesian) converge on a base case: FTTH overbuild reaches 75% of the U.S. addressable cable market by 2028, up from 55–60% today. In three-way overbuilt markets (fiber+fiber+cable with FWA share removed), each operator settles below 30% penetration. Fiber take rates currently average 46.5%; a 10% miss on take-rate assumptions turns marginal-IRR builds negative.

Why it matters: Gigapower's wholesale model hedges this risk only if tenant ISPs avoid the same overbuild traps. The Mesa/Chandler/Gilbert configuration is already a three-fiber market.

Action: JV finance rebuilds the IRR model with a 25%/30%/35% steady-state penetration scenario for three-fiber markets versus 45%/55% for two-fiber. Output review by June 12 before any new market authorization.
