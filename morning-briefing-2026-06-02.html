Policy / Regulatory

FCC pole-attachment rules took effect May 8 — 30-day contractor approval, deemed-granted default

19/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: Updated FCC pole-attachment rules went live May 8, 2026. Utilities must respond within 30 days to requests for new make-ready contractors or the request is deemed approved; attachers must give 60-day notice for orders above 300 poles; a new 120-day prep window covers orders of 3,000–6,000 poles.

Why it matters: Make-ready is the long pole in every Gigapower aerial build — the deemed-granted default converts utility foot-dragging into a schedule Gigapower controls.

Action: Direct Permitting + Engineering to re-file all pending make-ready requests across NV, AZ, PA under the new 30-day contractor clock this week, and pre-stage the 60-day notices for any 300+ pole runs queued for Q3.

Pennsylvania awards $1.16B BEAD — fiber-dominant, Comcast + Verizon take nearly half

20/22
Policy / Regulatory🔴 Act Today

What happened: Pennsylvania's $1.1618B BEAD allocation leans heavily to fiber-to-the-premises, with Comcast and Verizon together capturing close to half of all subgrant dollars. PA's broadband office named FTTP the preferred long-term technology.

Why it matters: Subsidized Comcast/Verizon fiber lands directly adjacent to Gigapower's Wilkes-Barre/Scranton (Luzerne/Lackawanna) build — a state-funded overbuild threat to take-rate and tenant-ISP economics.

Action: Have Wholesale BD and Government Affairs pull the PA county-level BEAD award map this week and overlay it on the Luzerne/Lackawanna footprint to flag census blocks where a subsidized competitor now changes the pass-vs-skip decision.

Nevada posts BEAD provisional awards — SkyFiber $57.5M, Stimulus $36.5M, Anthem $20.2M

19/22
Policy / Regulatory🟡 This Week

What happened: Nevada's $417M BEAD pool produced provisional awards led by SkyFiber Internet ($57.5M / 4,684 locations), Stimulus Technologies ($36.5M / 3,361), Anthem Broadband ($20.2M / 1,132), and DTS Fiber ($5.4M / 545).

Why it matters: Subsidized fiber entrants in Clark County and surrounding NV territory reshape the competitive map in Gigapower's Las Vegas market and could become either overbuilders or wholesale tenants.

Action: Task Wholesale BD to open exploratory wholesale-tenant conversations with Stimulus and Anthem before they commit to self-build electronics — convert a potential overbuilder into a Gigapower lit tenant.

Supply Chain

Glass sold out through 2026 — Corning locks $6B Meta deal as AI demand crowds out FTTH

20/22
Supply Chain🔴 Act Today

What happened: Industry reporting describes a "perfect storm" in fiber supply: at least one major glass maker has sold its entire 2026 fiber inventory, Corning is rumored to have stopped selling glass to other cablers, and Meta committed up to $6B to Corning through 2030 for AI data-center fiber. Tariffs further tighten overseas component sourcing.

Why it matters: AI hyperscalers are out-bidding FTTH operators for the same glass — Gigapower's 1.5M-location build target is exposed to allocation risk and price escalation if cable isn't locked now.

Action: Direct Construction + Procurement to convert 2026–2027 fiber-cable forecasts into firm POs with Corning/Prysmian/OFS this week, and open a second-source qualification with Sterlite/Fujikura before allocation tightens further.

Section 5 — My Recommendation

Lock 2026–2027 fiber-cable allocation this week — supply is the binding constraint, not demand. The glass market just inverted: at least one major manufacturer has sold its entire 2026 inventory, Corning is reportedly throttling third-party glass sales, and Meta's $6B commitment proves AI hyperscalers will out-bid every FTTH operator for the same product. At the same moment, Pennsylvania is handing Comcast and Verizon nearly half of a $1.16B BEAD pool to overbuild fiber adjacent to Gigapower's Wilkes-Barre/Scranton market, and Nevada's provisional awards seed new subsidized entrants in Clark County. Every one of those subsidized competitors will be drawing from the same constrained glass supply Gigapower needs. The operator that converts its 2026–2027 forecast into firm POs first — and qualifies a second source before allocation tightens — protects both build schedule and unit cost; the one that waits gets rationed. Direct Construction and Procurement to firm up Corning/Prysmian/OFS commitments and open Sterlite/Fujikura qualification now, before the JV's 1.5M-location target becomes a supply-gated promise instead of a plan.
