XGS-PON is now the default choice for new high-end FTTH; 25G-PON stays as premium overlay

XGS-PON (10G symmetric) has become the default PON technology for North American FTTH greenfield and upgrade programs. 25G-PON is a targeted overlay for premium residential tiers and selective enterprise/wholesale — not a footprint-wide choice. PON equipment market sized at $35.3B in 2025, forecast $61.5B by 2031 (9.7% CAGR).

Building GPON-only today prices you out of the 2–5 Gbps residential tier and kills your enterprise margin. XGS-PON is now table stakes.

Deploy now. Standardize on XGS-PON for every new OLT purchase starting this quarter — no exceptions. Reserve 25G-PON for selective multi-tenant/enterprise pilots only where the premium ARPU clears the incremental CapEx (why: mass deployment at 25G kills your build economics without corresponding uplift).

Smart home bundling — the new ARPU battleground for FTTH operators

ISPs are moving beyond connectivity into curated smart-home bundles (security, energy management, AI-enabled devices). Roughly 30M CPE units shipped in 2025 with embedded Zigbee/Thread radios. Operators are positioning as "full-stack smart-home gatekeepers."

Raw gig pricing is commoditizing. Bundled ARPU is how you defend against T-Mobile's mobile + fiber convergence and cable's pricing cuts — and it's defensible churn insulation.

Pilot test. Launch a single smart-home bundle (security cameras + smart thermostat + managed Wi-Fi) in your densest market within 90 days. Pick one vendor, one bundle, one price point — kill complexity (why: operators that try to build a catalog of 12 bundles end up selling nothing; the winners scale one offer before adding a second).
