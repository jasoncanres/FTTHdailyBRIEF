Corning fiber lead times balloon to 52 weeks; CommScope orders canceled

🔴 Act Today 20/22
Supply Chain
What happened

BEAD-winning ISPs report Corning loose-tube fiber is now on a 52-week lead time. Multiple ISPs have had CommScope cable orders canceled because Corning has stopped selling BABA-compliant glass to CommScope and others. AI data center demand is consuming fiber capacity ahead of FTTH builds.

Why it matters

Build schedules for any greenfield FTTH program touching 2026–2027 are now supply-constrained, not labor-constrained — and the bottleneck is at the glass layer, not the cabler.

Recommended action

Lock in 2027 cable allocations this week directly with Corning and OFS — do not rely on cabler intermediaries. Re-bid any open CommScope POs and add a BABA-compliance exit clause. Build a 90-day "diversion buffer" inventory for distribution cable on routes that are already permitted.

AI data centers are eating fiber inventory: 36× more fiber per rack vs. CPU

🔴 Act Today 20/22
Supply Chain / Market
What happened

AI racks (Blackwell 72-GPU nodes) require 16× the fiber of cloud switch racks; AI DCs overall consume ~36× the fiber of CPU racks. At least one major manufacturer has sold out of fiber inventory through 2026. The U.S. needs to add 213M more fiber miles by 2029 — more than doubling current ground.

Why it matters

FTTH operators are now competing for the same fiber, conduit crews, and splicing labor as hyperscale data center builders — and hyperscalers will outbid you.

Recommended action

Re-price every 2026 build pro forma at +12–18% on outside-plant materials. Pre-position fiber for the next two construction phases on-site before crews mobilize. Where DC clusters are nearby (Atlanta, Charlotte, Northern VA), move route engineering forward 60 days to avoid getting boxed out of conduit space.

XGS-PON dominant in NA; 25G/50G PON tested for premium tiers

🟡 This Week 18/22
Available Now
What's emerging

XGS-PON has won the U.S. greenfield platform decision. 25G-PON and 50G-PON are now in field tests for premium and dense urban deployments — expected mainstream after 2030. NG-PON2 is effectively dead as a strategic choice.

Why it matters for growth

Standardizing on XGS-PON now (not GPON, not NG-PON2) preserves a clean upgrade path to 25G with the same outside plant. That's capital efficiency on a 10-year horizon.

How to apply it

Deploy now. Lock in XGS-PON as the only acceptable 2026/2027 build standard. Reject any vendor proposal that doesn't include a stated 25G upgrade path on the same OLT chassis — that future capex hit will dilute your IRR.

MDU "PON-first" build pattern cuts per-user deployment cost 30–50%

🟡 This Week 18/22
Available Now
What's emerging

Industry consensus is now that MDU FTTH should default to a PON-first pre-build pattern: install fiber, splitters, and distribution frames during construction or initial activation, not on a unit-by-unit retrofit basis. Per-user deployment cost drops 30–50% vs. SDU comparables.

Why it matters for growth

MDU is the highest-margin FTTH segment in greenfield expansion. A 30–50% capex efficiency advantage over SDU lets you bid passings differently and fund deeper exclusive-access contracts with property owners.

How to apply it

Deploy now. Make MDU pre-build the default for any property of 24+ units in your pipeline, even where exclusive access isn't yet signed. Build a standardized 2-page MDU partnership term sheet (right of entry, marketing rights, revenue share) so sales doesn't slow on legal review.
