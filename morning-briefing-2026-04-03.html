1Fiber Supply Crunch: 138M km Shortfall, Prices Up 80%+
22 / 22 🔴 Act Today
Opportunity Score22 / 22
What Happened

Global fiber optic cable is in a structural supply crisis driven by AI data center buildouts. The 2026 global shortfall is estimated at 138 million fiber-kilometers (16.7% of demand unmet). Single-mode fiber prices have risen 80%+ since late 2025, with some grades up 400–650%; lead times for smaller buyers are approaching 12 months. North American demand growth is projected at 22–25%, while supply can only expand 12–19% due to 18–24 month preform manufacturing cycles.

Why It Matters

Every FTTH build you have planned for 2026–2027 is competing for the same constrained cable inventory being hoarded by hyperscalers funding AI infrastructure — your procurement window is closing.

Recommended Action

Lock in 2026–2027 cable contracts with Corning, CommScope, or a secondary supplier this week at fixed pricing. Negotiate volume commitments with a 12–18 month forward delivery window. If you don't have a dedicated procurement relationship with at least two vendors, you are already behind. Secondarily, audit your planned build topology — split-ratio optimization and reduced fiber counts can cut cable demand 15–25% without compromising capacity.

2BEAD Goes Tech-Neutral: Fiber Preference Eliminated, Cost Wins
21 / 22 🔴 Act Today
Opportunity Score21 / 22
What Happened

NTIA's June 2025 BEAD restructuring eliminated the program's fiber-first mandate. Subgrantee selection now favors lowest-cost-per-location bids regardless of technology — fixed wireless and LEO satellite compete on equal footing with fiber for "Priority" status. $42.5B is actively flowing with first construction expected to break ground in 2026; NC alone is awarding $300M+ under the new rules. A U.S. Senator publicly questioned spending $4,000/home on fiber when satellite costs $600, signaling political headwinds may intensify.

Why It Matters

Your competitive moat in BEAD-funded rural markets just eroded — fixed wireless and satellite operators can now undercut your fiber builds on per-location cost in competitive application rounds.

Recommended Action

Immediately rerun your BEAD target market cost models against the new lowest-cost-wins scoring criteria. Identify which of your planned service areas have cost-per-location above $2,500 — those face real competitive risk from FWA bids. For markets where fiber wins on cost, sharpen your application narrative to lead with TCO and 25-year infrastructure longevity, not speed tiers. For marginal markets, consider whether a hybrid fiber/FWA architecture preserves your application competitiveness. Get your state broadband office relationship primed now before the application windows open.

3Point Broadband + Clearwave Merger: New SE Fiber Giant, Aims to Double
21 / 22 🟡 This Week
Opportunity Score21 / 22
What Happened

Point Broadband (GTCR/Berkshire Partners-backed, 10-state fiber footprint) and Clearwave Fiber (Savannah, GA; Cable One JV) signed a definitive merger agreement closing H1 2026. The combined entity becomes one of the largest independent fiber operators in the U.S. — 12 states, 500K+ homes and businesses passed. The new CEO David Armistead has stated an explicit goal to double in size near-term through organic builds and M&A. The Southeast is their primary expansion corridor.

Why It Matters

You now have a well-capitalized, PE-backed competitor operating a scaled fiber platform explicitly hunting the same Southeast rural and suburban markets you're targeting — and they have a mandate to grow aggressively.

Recommended Action

Pull the combined entity's footprint map against your 2026–2027 build pipeline this week and flag every overlap market. For markets where you have no in-ground infrastructure yet, accelerate permitting and franchise agreements before Point/Clearwave's deployment teams get there first. If you have any markets where your cost position is weak or your build timeline is 18+ months out, evaluate whether to accelerate, partner, or cede. Also, assess whether the combined entity creates any near-term acquisition targets they will pick off — if so, move on them before the deal closes.
